C) GIPS DISCLOSURES REQUIREMENTS

1.     For firms that are verified

Verifications

a.       All the composite constructions

b.       The firm’s policies and procedures are designed to calculate and present performance in compliance with GIPS standards.

 

2.     For firms that have not been verified

 

Include that [NAME OF FIRM] has not been independently verified.

 

a.      Firms must disclose the composite creating date

b.      Firms must disclose that the firm’s list of composite descriptions is available upon request.

c.       Firms must disclose the definition of the firms

d.      Firms must disclose the composite description and the benchmark description

e.      Firms must disclose which measure of internal dispersion is used.

f.        Firms must disclose the presence, use, and extent of leverage, derivatives, and short positions, if material, including a description of the frequency of use and characteristics of the instruments sufficient to identify risks.

g.      Firms must disclose the periods of non-compliance (prior to 1/1/2000)

h.      Firms must disclose the use of a sub-advisor and the periods a sub-advisor was used.

i.        Firms must disclose if the 3-year annualized ex post standard deviation of the composite and/or benchmark is not presented because 36 monthly returns are not available

j.        If a custom benchmark is used, the firm must disclose the benchmark components, weights, and rebalancing process.

 

3.     Items must be reported for each composite

 

a.       At least 5 years of annual performance or since inception.  The firm must present additional annual performance up to a minimum of 10 years

b.       Annual returns: Gross OR Net

c.       The number of portfolio – not required if 5 or less (if more than 6, it is required)

d.       A measure of dispersion – not required if 5 or less.

e.       Three-year annualized ex post standard deviation using monthly returns

 

4.     Performance track record

Generally, a performance track record of a composite must stay with the firm where it was generated. 

 

There is an exception (3 conditions)

 

a.       The new firm takes all the investment decisions.

b.       The decision-making process remains substantially intact and independent

c.       The new firm has records that document and support the reported performance.
