B) COMPOSITE

 

1.     Basic compliance in composite creation

 

1.      A composite is an aggregation of discretionary portfolios that is managed according to a mandate, objective, or strategy. Firms are not permitted to include portfolios with different investment mandates, objectives, or strategies in the same composite.

 

2.      A portfolio becomes discretionary when the manager can implement the intended investment strategy

 

3.      If a portfolio status changes from discretionary to nondiscretionary, the portfolio may not be removed from a composite retroactively. But, the portfolio must be removed going forward.

 

4.      If a firm sets a minimum asset level for portfolios to be included in a composite, no portfolios below that asset level can be included in that composite.

 

5.      Terminated portfolios must be included in the historical returns of the appropriate composites up to the last full measurement period that the portfolio was under management

 

6.      Portfolios must not be switched from one composite to another unless documented changes in client guidelines or the redefinition of the composite make it appropriate.

 

7.      Significant external cash flows – the best practice is to put the cash in a temporary new account that is not part of the composite until the funds are invested in accordance with the style. 

 

8.      Carve outs: must not be included in a composite unless it is managed separately with its own cash balance.

 

2.     Characterics of discretionary include

 

a.       Client decides trades.

b.       Client can withdraw large amounts of cash.

c.       Restrictions on asset allocations or modify risk exposure.

d.       Relation is defined as "advisory".
