C) TAYLOR RULE AND MONETARY POLICY

 

1.     Taylor Rule:

 

The key variables watched by monetary authorities are as follows:

·         Economic growth rate.

·         Excess capacity in economy.

·         Unemployment Level

·         Inflation Rate.

 

Based on these parameters, economists determine whether economy is doing good or not or if there is any need to provide any stimulus.

 

Roptimal =Rneutral + 0.5 (Inflationforecast – Infationtarget) + 0.5 (GDPforecast – GPDtarget)

 

If economy is bad, lower interest rate to stimulate the economy. 

 

1.       If Economy's GPDtarget > GDPforecast then bank should reduce short term interest rate (Roptimal) to stimulate the economy and vice versa.

2.       If Inflation target > Inflation forecast then bank should reduce short term interest rate (Roptimal) to stimulate the economy and vice versa.

 

Monetary policy

Loose (Expansionary) Monetary policy means central bank can keep short term interest rate low and expand money supply to encourage borrowing by companies to stimulate the economy. . Vice versa for restrictive monitory policy.

 

2.     Policy mix and yield curve

 

Fiscal policy

Government actions (taxes subsidy and Govt. spending financed via budget deficit) to influence the economy.

Loose (Expansionary) Fiscal policy means Government can increase its spending and/or reduce taxes to stimulate the economy. Vice versa for restrictive fiscal policy.

 

1.       Monetary & Fiscal (both loose) – yield curve steep

2.       Monetary & Fiscal (both tight) – yield curve inverted

3.       Monetary (tight) & Fiscal (loose) – yield curve flat

4.       Monetary (loose)& fiscal (tight) – yield curve moderately steep

5.     Country risk analysis techniques

1.       Fiscal and monetary policy: the fiscal deficit to GDP >4% is a concern

2.       Economic prospects: annual growth rate of 4% or higher

3.       Competitive currency and external accounts: deficit to GDP over 4% is uncompetitive

4.       External debt: Foreign debt to GDP >50% is in dangerous territory

5.       Liquidity: Reserves to short term debt >200% safe, <100% a risky level.
