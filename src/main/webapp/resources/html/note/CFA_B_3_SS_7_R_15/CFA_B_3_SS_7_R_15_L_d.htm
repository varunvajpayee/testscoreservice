D) ECONOMIC FORECASTING

 

1.     Economic Forecasting models (Advantages /Disadvantages)

 

1.      Econometric Models Approach:

Advantages:

1) Robust

2) Once built, can be consistently used

3) Provide quantitative estimates

Disadvantages:

1) Most complex

2) Data inputs and relationships change

3) Poorly forecast recession

 

2.      Leading indicators:

Advantages:

1) Intuitive

2) May be available from third parties

3) May be tailored

Disadvantages:

1) Historically inconsistent

2) Can provide false signals

 

3.      Checklist approach:

Advantages:

1) Limited complexity

2) Flexible – easily incorporate structural change

Disadvantages:

1) Subjective

2) Time consuming

 

2.     Forecasting Exchange Rates 

 

Approaches to forecasting exchange rates:

 

1.      Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)

·   The exchange difference should offset any difference in the inflation rates between two countries.

·   PPP does seem useful in the long term, but not a useful guide to the direction of exchange rates in the short term.

·   Country with a higher inflation rate will see its currency depreciate.

 

2.      Relative Economic Strength

·   A strong economic growth attracts investment, creating demand for currency. As a result, the currency strengthens.

 

3.      Capital inflows forecasting approach

·   Net inflows into a country increases the demand for that country’s currency.  As a result, the currency strengthens.

 

4.      Saving-investment imbalances

·   Not easy to implement this approach, but explains why currencies may diverge from equilibrium values

·   If investment > domestic savings, capital from abroad must flow into the country to finance the investment.  To attract and keep the capital necessary for the savings deficit, the domestic currency must increase in value and stay strong.  

 

Government intervention

a.       Not very fruitful – government trading is too small in volume to affect the massive currency

b.       Currencies are more influenced by economic fundamentals than by periodic trading by government.
