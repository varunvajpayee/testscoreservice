BEHAVIORAL MODELS OF INVESTOR CLASSICATION

 

 

1.     BARNWELL TWO-WAY MODEL

 

Active Investors: They usually risked their own capital to gain wealth (Like Entrepreneurs).  Generally, they have a higher tolerance for risk than they have need for security. Generally, they are more comfortable, in situation where they feel in control.

 

Passive Investors: They have not risked their own capital to gain wealth but got wealthy passively. They may not know much about investing (risking capital). Most like they are long term savers or inheritors who save money during their jobs. Due to low risk tolerance, passive investors prefer a diversified portfolio.

 

 

 

2.     BAILHARD, BIEHL, &KAISER(BB&K) FIVE WAY MODEL

 

It divides the investors in five categories based on two dimensions, First (Y-Axis) Confidence level and Second (X-Axis) how careful they are in their investment decisions.

 

 

 

 

1.       The Adventurer:

a. Confident and Adventurers.

b. Generally, have undiversified portfolio because of habit of taking chances.

c. Likes to take their own decisions and reluctant to take advice, which presents challenges for investment advisers.

 

2.       The Celebrity:

a. Like to be the center of attention (Like celebrities).

b. Anxious and Impetuous.

c. Might hold opinions about some things but willing to seek and take advice about investing.

d. Aware of their limitations

 

3.       The Individualist:

a. Independent and careful but confident.

b. like to make their own decisions but only after careful analysis.

c. Rationally Listen and process information.

 

4.       The Guardian:

a. Cautious and Anxious.

b. Concerned about protecting their assets and future.

c.  May seek advice from those they perceive as being more knowledgeable.

 

5.       The Straight Arrow:

a. Sensible, secure, and balanced.

b. They fall at the intersection of two dimensions.

c. Willing to take risk in the expectation of earning a reasonable return.
