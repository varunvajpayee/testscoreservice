BEHAVIORAL FINANCE STRATEGIES AND LIMITATIONS

 

Following Behavioral finance strategies help in building an acceptable portfolio.

(Not an optimal portfolio as suggested in traditional finance).

 

 

1.     Goal Based Investing

 

Like Maslow's hierarchy of needs, investors financial goals are divided into layers (like Mental Account). So, the idea is to build the portfolio in layers

so, that each layer's return satisfies that layer's financial goal. Mental accounting bias, money is a fungible but it's still divided into layers and invested based on risk profile of that layer.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Limitations to Behavioral Classifications

1.       Individuals can display emotional and cognitive biases at the same time

2.       An individual may reflect traits of more than one behavioral investor type

3.       investors age and/or life circumstances will impact his/her risk tolerance.

4.       Two persons classified as the same investor type may need to be treated differently due to their unique behavior.

5.       Individuals tend to act irrationally at unpredictable times which limits the usefulness of behavioral finance model.
