A) MONITORING AND REBALANCING

1.     Calendar rebalancing:

Rebalancing a portfolio to its strategic allocation on a regular basis.

Advantages:

                                       i.            Provides a discipline.

                                     ii.            Does not need to monitor the portfolio within the rebalancing dates.

Disadvantages:

                                       i.            Could differ significantly from its optimal weights between rebalancing dates.

                                     ii.            Rebalancing is related to the passage of time, rather than the value of the portfolio.

 

2.     Percentage of portfolio: 

If weight is outside the band(corridor), the portfolio is rebalanced.

 

Advantages:

 

                                       i.            Exercise tighter monitoring.

                                     ii.            Rebalancing is related to the value of the portfolio.

 

3.     The optimal corridor width:

 

a.       Transaction costs: The higher transaction costs, less frequent rebalancing, thus wider optimal width.  Justification – the benefit should outweigh cost of rebalancing.

 

b.       Risk tolerance: The higher the risk tolerance, the lower the impact on the investor of deviation from target allocation weights.  Thus, it results in wider optimal corridors.

 

c.       Correlation of returns with other asset classes: The higher correlation, the wider corridor, When asset returns move together, the impact of deviations from target allocation is less.

 

d.       Volatility of asset class returns: Greater volatility make deviations from target weights more costly and suggest narrower optional corridor widths
