B) REBALANCING STRATEGIES IN DIFFERENT MARKETS

 

1.     Buy and hold(M=1):

“Do nothing” – no rebalancing is done. In a trend market, return from portfolio increases in same proportion as the equity returns. That's the reason slope is 1 and floor value is equal to the amount invested in risk free assets (which remains constant).

 

Portfolio value = M (Investment in stock + Floor Value), M =1

Payoff curve = Linear

Portfolio insurance = None

The investor’s risk tolerance is positively related to wealth and stock market returns.

 

 

 

2.     Constant Mix Strategy(M<1):

In a trend market, return from constant mix will be less than buy and hold, (hence M<1 and shape is concave) because funds are invested from equity to RF assets to maintain the constant mix. Floor value (RF assets) value can go to 0 in downward trend market.

 

Target investment in stock = M* Portfolio Value

There is no floor value

Portfolio insurance = Selling insurance

Multiplier: 0<M<1

Relative – constant

Concave – portfolio return increases at a decreasing rate. 

Perform well in a reversal market, but perform poorly in a trending market.

 

 

3.     CPPI (Constant Proportion Portfolio Insurance) (M>1):

In a trend market, return from constant mix will be more than buy and hold, (hence M>1 and shape is convex) because more funds are invested to equity, which are above the floor value. Since only cushion amount is used for equity investment so minimum portfolio value will be equal to the floor amount in the downward trend market.

 

Target investment in stock = M* (Portfolio value – Floor Value)

Multiplier: M>1

Portfolio insurance = buying insurance

Convex – portfolio return increases at an increasing rate

Perform best in a trend market, but perform poorly in a reversal market.
