D) ENDOWMENT

1 Return Objective:

(1) To maintain the real value of the investment portfolio

(2) To support [institution/University/Hospital]’s operating expenses over longer term in perpetuity.

(3) Required return: (1+annual spending) (1+Inflation rate) (1+mgmt fee)-1

(4) Spending rule:

·         Simple spending rule: Beginning Market Value * Spending %

·         Rolling 3-year average: (MVt-1 + MVt-2 + MVt-3)/3*Spending %

·         Geometric smoothing rate: Spending (t-1) *(1+Inflationt-1) *smoothing rate + Beg MKV t-1*spending rate* (1-smoothing rate)

 

Endowments that do not use a smoothing rule may have less tolerance for short-term portfolio risk due to volatility in returns.

 

2. Risk Tolerance:

·           As donation increases, risk tolerance increase as well.

·           As expected inflation increases, it results in increase in spending needs, thus lowering in risk tolerance.

·           Higher [institution/hospital/university etc.]’s dependence on funding from the endowment, Lower risk tolerance.

·           use of maximum contribution rule: higher risk tolerance.

·           use of smoothing rule: decrease in spending volatility, thus risk tolerance increases.

 

3. Constraints

·         Liquidity: Annual spending requirement + management fee – contributions

·         Time horizon: Single long term time horizon

·         Taxes: Tax-exempt (taxable for unrelated business income)

·         Legal: The prudent investor rule applies to foundations.  UMIFA is the regulatory body.
