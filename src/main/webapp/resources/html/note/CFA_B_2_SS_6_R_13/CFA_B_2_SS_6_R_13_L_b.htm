B) PENSION FUNDS

 

1.     Define Benefit (DB) vs Defined Contribution(DC)

1.      DC plan: e.g. ESOPs

·         The employee bears the investment risk.

·         The benefit not promised, so the plan sponsor does not recognize financial liability

·         Specifies the sponsor’s obligations in terms of contributions to the pension fund

·         Readily portable

·         Just provide a menu of investment options, no need to set investment return objective and constraints.

2. DB plan – e.g. a cash balance plan.

·         The employer bears the investment risk.

·         A pension plan that specifies the plan sponsor’s obligations in terms of the sponsor’s    obligations in terms of the benefit to plan participants.

·         DB plan participants bear early termination risk. 

Accumulated Benefit Obligation (ABO) is the PV of obligations already owed to workers.

Projected Benefit Obligation (PBO) Is equal to ABO + the PV of additional liability that will accumulate from expected future compensation provided the benefits are tied to a quantity such as final average pay.

Total future liability is the PV of accumulated and projected future service benefits, including the effects of projected future compensation increases. It's most comprehensive but most uncertain measure of pension liability because model inputs are subjective and difficult to measure.

2.     Return objective:

·         to generate return sufficient to meet pension liabilities

·         If fully funded, minimum return requirement = discount rate of plan liabilities.

 

3.     Risk objective:

 

·          Increase in sponsor’s financial status, contribution and profitability also increases the Risk tolerance.

·          The greater the strength of the sponsor, the greater the plan’s risk tolerance.

·          Sponsor’s good financial status indicates an ability to increase plan contribution.

·          Higher plan’s surplus, plan’s income, greater risk tolerance

·          Plan’s features: -early retirements & lump sum withdrawals =) lower risk tolerance because       they increase liquidity needs and lower time horizon. 

·          Sponsor & Pension fund’s common risk exposure: lower is better (higher risk tolerance)

·          Lower correlation indicates an ability to increase plan contribution if fund’s return is poor.

·         Workforce characteristics:  Higher risk tolerance as A) younger age of workforce & B) lower inactive to active ratio =) longer time horizon.

·         Closed to new participants: Liquidity requirement increases & duration goes down. This results in lower risk tolerance

·         No inflation indexing – risk tolerance goes up as future payments to current retirees will not increase.

 

 

4.     Constraints:

Liquidity

It's needed due to

(A) increase in retired lives,

(B) decrease in sponsor’s contribution

(C) existence of plan features.

Tax

Most pension funds are tax exempt.

Legal/Regulatory

Pensions are subject to ERISA—the employee retirement income security act.
