E) INSURANCE COMPANY

 

1.     Policy Types

 

An insurance company’s primary investment objective is to fund future policyholder benefits and claims.

 

Whole/Variable life- Insurance premium contribute to the cash value. You can take out money as cash value or get value at the time of death.

 

Term life-  Insurance coverage on a year to year basis

2.     Return Objective:

Companies tries to balance the risk of their obligations with their return. For whole life policies have return requirement of

= Credited interest rates + net interest spread

 

3.     Risk Objective

 

a.       Valuation risk: As interest goes up, value of assets and liabilities decrease.  When duration of asset is greater than duration of liability, the value of asset decreases more than the value of liability =) surplus decreases.

b.       Disintermediation risk: as market interest goes up, disintermediation risk goes up. 

Results – increase in liquidity requirement, decrease in liability duration, and thus decrease in risk tolerance. 

c.       Cash flow volatility risk: As interest goes up, prepayment rate goes down, result in slower cash flows, then lead to lower return (negative effect)

d.       Credit Risk: As credit spread widens, it will result in lower valuations & indicates an increase in default risk. Thus, asset value and surplus will go down.

e.       As interest rate goes down, reinvestment risk will occur -income from the portfolio can be reinvested at the rates lower then the original coupon rate.

 

4.     Constraints

 

Liquidity

o    Surrender clause & disintermediation: They causes liquidity need goes up, time horizon goes down, thus risk tolerance goes down. Compared to non-life insurance companies, life insurance companies have much lower liquidity requirements due to the greater predictability

 

Tax Concerns

Generally, Insurance companies are tax-paying partially or wholly. In USA, companies are taxed on return above to actuary minimum defined return.
