G) BANKS

 

Banks are financial intermediaries involved in taking deposits and lending money.

A bank’s asset/liability risk management committee (ALCO) is generally in charge of overseeing the bank’s securities portfolio. ALCO measure profitability indicators like (Net Interest Margin, Interest spread or VaR- Value at risk).

 

 

Banks’ objectives in managing securities portfolios include the following-

 

1.       To manage overall interest rate risk of the balance sheet. (Market Risk)

2.       To manage liquidity. (Liquidity Risk)

3.       To produce income. (Operating risk)

4.       To manage credit risk. (Credit Risk)

 

 

1.     Return

The return objective is to earn a positive interest rate spread above the cost of funds.

 

2.     Risk Objectives

Banks uses ALM framework to meet the liquidity requirements for their liabilities (deposits made by the customers) so they have below-average risk tolerance

 

3.     Liquidity Requirements

 

Portfolio should be liquid to meet loans and regulatory requirements.

 

4.     Time Horizon

Time horizon is short (3-7years) linked to the short-term liability.

 

5.     Legal and Regulatory Factors

 

Due to Risk-based capital (Basel accords) regulations, they are required to have certain amount of liquidity available all the time. Regulations place restrictions on banks’ holdings of common shares and below-investment-grade risk fixed-income securities.
