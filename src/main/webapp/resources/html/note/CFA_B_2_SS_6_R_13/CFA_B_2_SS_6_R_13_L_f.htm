F) NON-LIFE INSURANCE COMPANY

 

1.     Non-life insurance

(1) More uncertain regarding the amount and timing.  Thus, lower risk tolerance,

(2) Shorter duration & longer tail (takes long to process claims)

(3) Also, exposed to inflation risk because replacement item value may increase due to inflation.

 

 

2.     Return

The main factors that affect the return objective are

·         Competition Pricing- Low insurance policy premium due to competition.

·         Profitability of the underlying insurance company.

·         Growth of surplus is needed to grow the business.

 

3.     Risk

 

They generally have average to below-average risk tolerance because 

 

·         Cash flow characteristics.  can be quite erratic.

·         Common stock to surplus ratio- Regulators limits the investment of surplus into common stock.

 

4.     Time Horizon

Since duration of their liabilities is shorter so time horizon is shorter.

 

5.     Taxes

Taxable entities.

 

6.     Liquidity

Due to short duration of liability, liquidity requirement for Non-life insurance company is significant. Liquidity remains important for these companies for providing portfolio flexibility under changing tax, underwriting, and interest rate conditions.

 

7.     Legal/Regulatory

 A casualty company is not required to maintain an asset valuation reserve but risk-based capital requirements are increasingly common.

 

 

 Mutual funds (investment companies) and hedge funds are investment intermediaries, whereas foundations, endowments, insurance companies, and banks are financial intermediaries. Compared with individual investors, they all usually have larger amounts of money to invest.
