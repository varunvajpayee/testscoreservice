C) FOUNDATION

Foundations are typically grant-making institutions funded by gifts and investment assets. (For example: Gates Foundation).

1.     Types of foundation

·         Independent -  Private or Family based.

·         Community - Public sponsored organization.

·         Company Sponsored - Closed ties with the corporate providing funds.

·         Operating - Uses own resources to operate (like Museums).

2.     Return objective:

·         To maintain the real value of the investment portfolio

·         To meet its minimum return requirement

 

Minimum return requirement = Annual spending requirements +Inflation rate +Management fees

 

Multiplicative: (1+annual spending) (1+inflation) (1+mgmt fee)-1

 

3.     Risk objective:

Higher contribution/donations, higher risk tolerance

Time horizon: long term – higher risk tolerance

 

4.     Constraints:

1.      Liquidity

Generally private foundations have a minimum 5% spending rate on that year’s average asset value (by the Tax Reform Act of 1969) because it must maintain an asset reserve. Following can be used to drive liquidity requirements.

Annual spending rate + management fee – contribution

2.      Time horizon

Mostly infinite time horizon (single long term horizon)

3.      Taxes

Tax exempt, but taxable for unrelated business income.

4.      Donation

Donation can be received in terms on single large equity portfolio (Concentrated Position).
