Six components of the Asset Manager code of conduct: -

 

1.    Loyalty to Client

a.       Place the client interest ahead of the firm's interest.

b.       Manage Client’s confidentiality. (Example -AML policy to prevent involvement in illegal activities.)

c.       Refuse business relationships and gifts that would compromise independence and objectivity and loyalty to clients. -

(Gifts from 3rd party can be more problematic than client's gifts).

2.    Investment Process and actions

a.       Reasonable care and judgement

b.       Do NOT manipulate price and volume to mislead market participants.

(Intension should be correct. Selling a thinly traded stock (in interest of client) is correct (even if It impacts the market) as long as intentions are good to help the client)

c.       Deal fairly with all clients-

(Higher compensation for higher level of service to some clients is fine and different requirement (Asset base > 200m) to qualify for service is fine as well).

d.       Have reasonable and adequate basis.

e.       Portfolio managed to a specific style and strategy generally do not have to evaluate the suitability to a given client.  In fact, the client should let manager know whether a style and strategy doesn't suit him.

f.        Managing portfolio of a specific client- As per the IPS.

 

3.    Trading

a.       Do not act or cause other to act on MNI

b.       Give Client priority over the firm-  manager may invest their own capital along with clients, if clients do not suffer.

c.       Use client's soft dollar for client's benefits.

d.       Seek best execution for client's trade (Not necessary the best price).

e.       Establish policies for fair and equitable trade allocation (Pro-rate basis)- Not on adhoc basis.

f.        Use of a block trade (clubbing trades of various client at an average price) is acceptable.

 

4.    Risk Management Compliance and support.

 

a.       Develop desired Plans and procedures to comply with the AMC and regulatory dept.

b.      Appoint a compliance officer (eligible).

c.       3rd party to verify the information provided to the clients to check whether it is accurate and complete.

d.       Maintain the records of investment actions.

e.       Employ sufficient and qualified stage to meet all AMC requirements.

f.        Annual certification by the employee that they are familiar with the compliance procedure.

g.       Develop Business continuity plan

h.       Establish firm wide level risk management plan.

 

5.    Performance and Valuation/Disclosure (as per the GIPS).

 

a.       The AMC recommends that “managers should report to clients at least quarterly, and when possible, such reporting should be provided within 30 days after the end of the quarter.” [Even during lock up period]

b.       The AMC also states that “at a minimum, Managers should provide clients with gross- AND net-of-fees returns

c.       The Asset Manager Code, managers should disclose to each client the actual fees and other costs charged to them, together with itemizations of such charges, when requested by clients. The disclosure should include the specific management fee, incentive fee, and the amount of commissions paid on clients

 

6.    Disclosures

 

a.       Proper disclosures of conflict of interest and past performance history.

b.       Proper disclosures related to compensation.
