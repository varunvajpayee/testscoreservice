Points

 

1.       People with Corporate finance background are not suited to become asset manager. Consequently, they may not act with skill or competence, as required by the fourth principle of the General Principles of Conduct. Professional asset managers should be hired to manage the Fund.

2.       In case there exists a relationship between investment banking department and investment management departments of the firm then it should be disclosed to the client (if investment banking client money is invested in Investment management fund).

3.       the level and complexity of business-continuity planning depends on the size, nature, and complexity of the organization involved. If it's a large firm then it should have alternative plans for monitoring, analyzing, and trading investments if primary systems become unavailable.

4.        Asset Manager Code, managers must disclose to prospective clients the average or expected expenses or fees clients are likely to incur, and to existing clients the actual fees and other costs charged to them.

5.       3rd Party verifies the claim of compliance (not the CFA institute).

6.       Under AMC, both gross of fees return and net of fees return must be disclosed.

IPS should be reviewed annually and portfolio performance should be reported to clients quarterly.

 

 

Asset Management Code of Conduct:

 

1.       Voluntary

2.       Applies to investment management firm.

3.       Comply to the strictest rule (in case asset manager compliant firm has its own rule).
