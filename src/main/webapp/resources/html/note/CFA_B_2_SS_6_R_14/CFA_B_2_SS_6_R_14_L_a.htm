A) LINKING PENSION LIABILITIES TO ASSETS

 

Inactive Participants -These are the benefits attributable to retired employees currently receiving pension payments.

Active Participants: Current employees who are contributing to pension fund.

Deferred Participants: Former employees, who are not receiving any benefits but their benefits have accrued.

 

1.     Pension liability mimicking asset types (Modelled Risk):

For modelled Risk, Assets (portfolio) return can be used to pay off the liabilities. Here is the mapping between liabilities and the modelled portfolio.

a.      Liability for inactive pension plan participants: Nominal bond portfolio

b.      Liability for accrued benefits of deferred participants : Nominal bond portfolio

c.       Liability for future benefits of active pension plan participants:

§ Future real wage growth: stocks

§ Future wage growth due to inflation: inflation indexed bonds (real rate bonds)

d.      If plan is frozen: only accrued benefits, thus only nominal bond portfolio.

e.      Closing to new participants: nominal (existing in-active), nominal (existing active), and real rate bonds + equities (existing active – future benefits).

f.        If the fund is less mature & high proportion of active members – increase equities.

 

2.     Liability Noise (Non-Modelled Risk)

Liability noise involves risks that cannot be modelled, and hence are very difficult to manage.

Noise for Inactive participants (model uncertainty)-

 

A) Inactive Participants: Longevity risk

B) Deferred Participant: retirement date risk + longevity risk

C) active employees: Future benefits payment to active employees is based on assumption like withdrawal, disability, and retirement, and therefore are embedded with a large amount of uncertainty.

 

3.     AO (Asset Only Portfolio) VS ALM (Asset Liability Management)

AO (Asset Only Portfolio) Approach

a.       Do not consider liability exposure.

b.       Goal is to maximize the asset's return.

c.       Cash is considered as a risk-free return.

d.       Emphasis is on assets diversification (low correlation among assets) to minimize the risk.

ALM (Asset Liability Management)

a.       Does consider of liability exposure.

b.       Goal is to cover the liability exposure.

c.       Liability mimicking asset portfolio is considered as a safe investment.

d.       Emphasis is on asset's high correlation with liability to minimize the risk.

4.     Points

a.       For a pension plan, typically consists mostly of regular bonds, along with some stocks and a small amount of inflation indexed bonds.

b.      A more mature pension plan should have a higher allocation to regular bonds, and thus a smaller allocation to stocks and inflation-indexed bonds, than a less mature pension plan.

c.       If the pension plan has full inflation indexing, the regular bonds should be replaced with inflation indexed bonds (real rate bonds).
