A)  ESTATE PLANNING

 

It's a planning process for transferring the wealth to others. In form of:

 

·         Gifts (lifetime gifts

·         Bequest (At death).

 

1.     Probate

is the legal process to determine the validity of decedent's will.

 

Advantages to avoiding probate process.

 

a.       Probate causes high court fee.

b.       Probate process can cause delay.

c.       Many problems can arise when multiple jurisdictions are involved.

 

A decedent without a valid will is considered to have died intestate (states decides the property distribution).

 

2.     Ownership rights

 

A) Forced Hiership rules

Children have rights to fixed share of parent's estate. Wealthy individual might try to avoid forced Hiership rules by:

·         Gifting away assets to others.

·         Moving assets into a trust.

 

Claw back provision:

The child may be able to recover his/her forced share from the ones who received the lifetime gifts.

 

B)  A Community property regimes

 

Each spouse receives 50% of total estate that was built up during the marriage.

Gifts and inheritance received are exclude,

 

Spouse share is limited to the greater of the amount as per the community property regime or as forced heir ship rules.

 

C) Separate property regimes

Each source is entitled for his/her property.

4.     Minimizing tax strategies

 

·         Generation Skipping

By transferring assets directly to the third generation (i.e., grandchildren) or beyond may reduce transfer taxes where permitted. Saving is around (1/(1-t)).

 

·         Spouse Exceptions

Some jurisdictions allow transfer of assets to a surviving spouse without tax consequences, however it's still valuable to transfer that amount to the next generation without the estate tax.

 

·         Valuation Discounts

It's difficult to value partnerships or small business, discount for illiquid and discount for control is usually applied on the discount rate. It's efficient from taxation perspective because cost bases is reduced because of these discounts.

 

·         Charitable Gifts

 Assets can be transferred to charitable organizations in tax efficient manner (Low taxes or not taxed on capital gains).

6.     Trusts

 

Benefits of a Trust

·         Control - By putting restrictions on distribution of assets, Grantor can exercise control.

·         Asset Protection: In an irrevocable trust the assets are safe from creditors

·         Tax Reduction: trusts can be tax efficient wealth transfer vehicles in certain jurisdictions.

 

Revocable Trust

The grantor can rescind the trust and regain title to the trust. He/She is the legal owner and subject to paying taxes because of this, creditor can make claim again the grantor's trust assets.

 

Irrevocable Trust

The trustee may be responsible for tax payments. The grantor cannot rescind the trust. Since grantor is no longer the legal owner of trust's assets so creditors cannot claim on trust's assets.

 

Fixed Trust

The trust document determines the amount of distribution and timing of distribution of funds.

 

Discretionary Trust

Trustee can provide distributions as per their discretion.

 

Life Insurance

Life Insurance can be an efficient way to transfer wealth as well. Death benefit proceeds paid to life insurance beneficiaries are tax exempt. Life insurance premium paid is not subject to wealth transfer tax and life Insurance can be used to avoid the probate process.

 

 

7.     Tax Jurisdictions

 

A country that taxes all income generated within its borders imposes source jurisdiction tax system (territorial tax system). Countries may also impose tax based on residency, called residence jurisdiction. Following three types of conflicts may arise:

 

·         Residence-Residence conflicts arise when two countries claim the same person's residence.

·         Source-Source conflicts arise when two countries claim the source jurisdiction the same asset.

·         Residence-Source conflicts arise when asset is  in one country but the owner is in another country.

 

 

Methods to alleviate double taxation problem

 

A) Credit method

the residence country give tax credit for taxes paid to a foreign country. The credit is limited to the amount of taxes the taxpayer would pay in the resident country, which eliminates double taxation.

 

Effective Tax Rate = Max [TResidence, TSource]

 

B) Exemption method

the residence country exempts income earned in Source country for tax purpose, which eliminates the double taxation problem.

 

Effective Tax Rate =TSource

 

Tax liability is equal to taxes paid at the foreign source country.

 

C) Deduction method

the residence country allows tax deduction for taxes paid to the source country. The taxpayer is still responsible for both taxes, but the aggregate liability is less than the sum of the two with the residence country reducing the size of its percentage claim by the product of the two tax rates.  It offers partial solution to the double taxation problem.

 

 

Effective Tax Rate =TResidence + Tsource - TResidence * Tsource
