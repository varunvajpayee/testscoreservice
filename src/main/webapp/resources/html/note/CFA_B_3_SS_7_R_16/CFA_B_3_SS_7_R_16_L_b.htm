B) RELATIVE EQUITY MARKET VALUATION

 

1.     The Fed Model

 

If below ratio is greater than (less than) 1 then equity market is underpriced(overpriced).

 

 

𝑺&𝑷 𝒆𝒂𝒓𝒏𝒊𝒏𝒈𝒔 𝒚𝒊𝒆𝒅 < 𝑻𝒓𝒆𝒂𝒔𝒖𝒓𝒚 𝒚𝒊𝒆𝒍𝒅 =  Overpriced

𝑺&𝑷 𝒆𝒂𝒓𝒏𝒊𝒏𝒈𝒔 𝒚𝒊𝒆𝒅 > 𝑻𝒓𝒆𝒂𝒔𝒖𝒓𝒚 𝒚𝒊𝒆𝒍𝒅 =  Underpriced

 

Advantages

a.       Easy to understand and apply.

b.       Consistent with DCF model/

 

Drawbacks

a.       Ignores the equity risk premium

b.       Compares a real variable with nominal (Forward earning yield is real whereas US treasury bond yield is nominal).

c.       Ignores earning growth.

 

 

 

2.     The Yardeni Model

As per GGM model:

P0= E1/r-g

P0/E1 = 1/r-g

E1/P0 = r - g

Since g= d(LTEG) and r = Tb.

 

E1 = Next year’s earnings estimate

P0 = Current year’s price

Yb = Yield on A rated corporate bonds

d = Importance of earnings growth

LTEG = 5-year growth forecast

 

Market is undervalued when below condition is true otherwise market is overvalued.

 

Advantages

a.       Improvement on the fed model by using the Yield on A rated corporate bond as required return on equity.

 

Drawbacks

a.       Risk Premium- largely a default risk premium (Not equity risk premium).

b.       d is an arbitrary assumed number.

 

3.     10-Year Moving Average P/E ratio (aka Cyclically Adjusted P/E Ratio (CAPE))

 

It is "real S&P 500 price index" divided by moving average of the preceding 10 years of real reported earnings. “Real” denotes that the stock index and earnings are adjusted for inflation using the Consumer Price Index (CPI).

 

= Real S&P 500 price index / Moving average of the preceding 10 years of real reported earnings

 

 

4.     Asset Based Model

 

Tobin’s q

 

Market value of its debt and equity divided by the replacement cost of its assets.

 

 

Equity q

 

Equity market capitalization divided by net worth measured at replacement cost.
