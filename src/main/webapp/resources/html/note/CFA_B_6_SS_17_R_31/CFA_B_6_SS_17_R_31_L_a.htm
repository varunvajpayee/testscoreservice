A) COMPONENTS OF PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

 

1.     Components

Performance evaluation into three components:

·         Performance measurement (Calculate rate of return during the measurement period)- Quantify the performance.

·         Performance attribution (Performance sources relative to the benchmark)- Find out key performance contributors.

·         Performance appraisal (Evaluating skill of the manager)-Draw conclusions based on analysis.

 

2.     Evaluating Portfolio Performance

a.      Return calculation:

b.      Time Weighted vs Money Weighted Returns

 

TWRR

It is the compound growth rate over a period. Rate of return is recalculated whenever there is an external cash flow.

 

𝑇𝑊𝑅𝑅= (1+𝑟1)(1+𝑟2)…(1+𝑟n)−1

Advantages:

1.       Not affected by external cash flows, so it can evaluate the return of assets.

2.       Required under GIPS.

Disadvantages:

 1) data intensive and expensive to perform

 

MWRR

It is the IRR on all funds invested during a period.

 

Advantages:

1.       Only requires a beginning and end of period of market value

2.       Useful in evaluating returns when the investment manager maintains control and timing of cash flow.

Disadvantages:

1.       It considers of all cash flow (external ones also).

Note: If funds are contributed to an account prior to a period of strong performance: MWR greater than TWR.
