A) CONCENTRATED POSITIONS

 

1.     The three asset classes with most concentration risk

1.       publicly traded single-stock positions.

2.       privately held businesses (including family-owned businesses), and

3.       investment real estate.

 

2.     Possible reasons behind having a concentrated single asset Position

1.       Restriction on sale (restricted stock)

2.       A desire to control.

3.       High return expectation.

4.       Asset may have other uses.

 

3.     Investment Risks of Concentrated Positions (>25% of position RULE OF THUMB)

1.       Systematic Risk (market risk).

2.       Idiosyncratic Risk (Company specific risk).

3.       Property specific risk for real estate properties

 

4.     Objectives in dealing with concentrated positions

1.       Reduce the risk

2.       Generate liquidity

3.       Optimize tax efficiency- maximize the after-tax value.

 

5.     Institutional and Capital Market Constraints on an investor's ability to reduce a concentrated position

1.       Margin lending rule- Margin rules determine how much a bank or brokerage firm can lend against securities positions that their customers own.

2.       Securities Laws and Regulations - Hinders one's ability to liquidate an asset on time.

3.       Contractual Restrictions and Employer Mandates (Employee lock up period)

4.       Capital Market Limitations.

 

6.     Managing concentrated position

1.       Outright sale

2.       Monetization - Borrow funds to invest without a tax event due to sale.

3.       Hedging (with the use of derivatives)

 

7.     Goal Based Investing

Separating assets into three buckets-

1.       Personal risk bucket - Goal is to protect from poverty and a dramatic fall in life style.

2.       Market risk bucket - Average risk-adjusted market return by investing in stocks and bonds to sustain the life style.

3.       Aspirational bucket - This is where concentrated position is placed.

 

Primary capital (Primary Goals) =  personal risk bucket + market risk bucket

Surplus capital (Secondary Goals) = Aspirational Risk bucket

 

8.     Considerations when deciding on monetization instruments.

1.       Taxations.

2.       Counterparty Credit Risk

3.       Ability to Close Out Transaction Prior to Stated Expiration.

4.       Price Discovery

5.       Transparency in fees

6.       Flexibility in terms.

 

9.     Ways to Hedge

1. Protective put (Expensive strategy because of In the money put option is bought)

2. Knock-out put (Relatively Cheaper than protective put)

3. ATM and OTM puts (Cheaper because of buying at the money put and selling out of money put)

4. Put and Call (Cheaper because of buying put and selling call at same exercise price)

5. Prepaid variable forward (PVF): It's used to lock in their profits and defer their taxes. Investor gets money in advance for delivering the stocks   sometime in future. It is structured so that the number of shares owed back decreases as the stock price rises so that investor can retain some upside.

 

10.Ways to Monetize

1.        Short against the box- Investor short his/her own shares.  This results in a neutral position where your gains in a stock are equal to the losses.

2.        Forward sale: If there is a forward contract present of the stock (which Investor has concentrated position) then he can sell the forward contract.

3.        Forward with options: If options exist then sell the call option of the stock.

4.       Total equity swap - Swap stocks return with the return of a fixed income instrument.

5.       Yield Enhancement - Writing covered calls against some or all the shares. investor does retain stock’s downside exposure (losses offset by the amount of premium received) and capped the upside potential.

 

11.Financial Tool Set for Managing Private Business Equity

Strategies that are available to allow company owners to generate full or partial liquidity include

1.       Sale to 3rd party investor: Buyer could be a Strategic buyer, Financial buyer or other investors.

2.       Sale to insider like Management or employees (like employee stock options plan).

3.       Recapitalization (Sell some portion of the concentrated stock)

4.       Sell non-core assets (often real estate)

5.       Loan or Personal line of credit against company shares.

6.       Initial public offering.

 

12.Monetization Strategies for Real Estate Owners

1.       Mortgage Financing.

2.       Transfer to Donor advised fund or charitable trust.

3.       Sale & Leaseback.
