D) MANAGING MARKET RISK

 

1.     Stress Testing

 

Stress testing involves construction of extreme market conditions scenarios and verify whether firm or business can sustain in those scenario market conditions. It helps firm to assess their potential weak areas, loss concentration and their risk positions. It's conducted by the firm on regular basis (or sometimes on adhoc basis) as part of their risk management process.

 

a.      Actual even scenarios - Actual Scenarios of the past

b.      Hypothetical Scenarios - Hypothetical scenarios

c.       Stylized scenario -CCAR is a type of Stylized scenario (Multi factors)- (Common in finance industry). Stylized scenario is a simulated movement in interest rates, exchange rates, stock prices and /or commodity price, which are common in finance industry.

 

Scenario stressing models

 

a.      Factor push analysis- analyst push some factors to their extreme values

b.      Maximum loss optimization- mathematical approach to find worst combination of factors.

c.       Worst case scenario-all factors are pushed to their extreme values.

 

 

2.     Limit Management

 

To control risk exposure of the firm, limits are placed at various levels (budget, exposure, product or at business unit levels).

 

Examples:

 

·         Performance Stop outs Maximum amount that a given portfolio can lose in a period (e.g., a month or a year).

·         Working Capital Allocations limit.

·         VaR Limits

·         Risk Factor Limits

·         Position Concentration Limits - Enforce diversification.

·         Leverage Limits A maximum amount of leverage in the portfolio may be specified.

·         Liquidity Limits
