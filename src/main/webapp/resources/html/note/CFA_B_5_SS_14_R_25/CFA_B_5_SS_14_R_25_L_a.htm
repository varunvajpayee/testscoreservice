A) RISK MANAGEMENT PROCESS AND SYSTEM

 

1.     Risk Management Process

 

Risk Management Process involves

 

a.       Identifying the level of risk that an entity wants.

b.       Taking actions that bring the actual level of risk to the desired level of risk.]

c.       Hedge the risk when company doesn't have a comparative advantage in an activity or line of business.

 

The risk management process is as follows:

1.       Set policies and procedures,

2.       Define risk tolerance,

3.       Identify risks,

4.       Measure risks, and

5.       Adjust the level of risk.

 

2.     System of risk management

 

a.      Centralized risk control system

Centralized enterprise wide risk control systems responsible to all business unit.

Advantage

                                       i.            Brings risk control closer to the key decision makers in the organization

                                     ii.            Better management of risk budget by recognizing the diversification across business units and providing the centralized solution to all business unit.

 

b.      Decentralized system

Each business unit is responsible for its own risk control.

 

Advantage

                                       i.            Placing risk control in nearer proximity to the source of risk taking.

Disadvantage

                                       i.            Not accounting for portfolio effects across units.

 

Separate Risk Management Team

 

There should be clear separation of front office and back office duties. Individual who are independent of business should be responsible to do the risk management. Centralized system is preferred over the decentralized system.
