B) INTERNATIONAL INVESTING

 

 

1.      Risk (Cost) in International Investing

 

a.       Currency Risk - Risk due to change in exchange rate.

b.       Political Risk- Instable Government, weak Judiciary process (Legal Structure) and weak fiscal/monetary policy contribute to political risk.

c.       Home Country Preference - Investors are familiar with home country so they prefer to invest in home country market over international market.

d.       Liquidity - Emerging market are less liquid so they have higher bid-ask spread.

e.       Higher transaction cost and fees

 

 

2.     International diversification

Benefits of investing in international market.

 

·         Increases stock prices- stock prices usually increase as investors buy equities that were previously unavailable.

·         Decline Expected return: As prices rise in new market, expected return decline in new market.

·         Decreases volatility-As the economy matures, return variability may decline over the long run.

·         Decrease diversification benefits

·         Improves market microstructure - In long run, transactions costs are low, trading volumes are high and better information efficiency.

·         increases informational efficiency,

·         lowers the cost of capital

·         increases economic growth.

 

 

2.     International diversification during crisis time (contagion)

 

At the time of crisis, diversification benefits of investing in emerging market are disappeared (when they are needed the most).  At the time of crisis, market volatility increases correlation between assets in developed and developing countries.so Global market converge and the benefits of investing in global market disappear.

 

3.     Growth in emerging economy

 

There are several explanations why emerging market growth does not translate into higher equity returns:

·         Stock prices reflects future growth already.

·         Private companies, government-owned companies, and subsidiaries of foreign companies are benefited the most.

·         Demand of high wages by employees.

·         Management may not work in the best interest of shareholders (weak corporate governance).
