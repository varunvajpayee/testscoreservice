C) APPROACHES TO ASSET ALLOCATION (OPTIMIZATION)

 

1.     Mean Variance Analysis

Involves selecting the efficient portfolio that best satisfies the investor’s risk, return objective and constraints.

Advantage

                                      i.            Optimization programs used to generate the efficient frontier are inexpensive and readily available.

                                     ii.            Widely accepted and understood.

Limitation

                                       i.            Drawback – estimation error (estimating the expected return is difficult).

Adjacent corner portfolio:

·         Used when return objective is greater than tangency portfolio & no short is allowed. 

·         Tangency portfolio is the portfolio with the highest sharp ratio. 

·         The GMV (Global Minimum Variance) is included as a corner portfolio irrespective of its asset weights.

·         Knowing the composition of the corner portfolios allows us to compute the weights of any portfolio on the minimum variance frontier.

2.     Resampled Efficient Frontier

Takes into account of estimation error

Advantage:

a.       Tend to be more stable

b.       Tend to be more well diversified

Limitation:

a.       No theoretical basis for the approach.

b.       Inputs are often based on historical data.

 

3.     The Black-Litterman approach

·         Reverse engineers the expected returns implicit in a diversified market portfolio.

·         Combines those expected returns with the investor’s own views & takes into account the investor’s confidence. 

Advantage:

a.       It starts with a diversified portfolio.

Limitation:

a.       Complex and typically depends on historically based volatility estimates

 

4.     Monte Carlo Simulation

·         Evaluate a proposed asset allocation’s multi-period performance.

·         Cash inflows and outflows, taxes and transaction costs can be modeled in a simulation.

Advantage:

a.       Models path dependency issues

b.       Can also model liabilities and surplus

Limitations:

a.       Can be complex to implement

b.       Can generate false confidence. 

 

5.     ALM approach

Focuses on optimizing with respect to net worth or surplus.

Advantage

a.       Considers the allocation of assets with respect to liabilities

 

6.     Experience based approach

Long time horizons allow for higher risk

A 60/40 allocation of equity/bonds is a good starting point for an average investor

Advantage:

a.       Easy to understand and consistent with the more complex approaches.

b.       Inexpensive to implement.

Limitations:   

a.       Allocation rules may be too simple for some investors.
