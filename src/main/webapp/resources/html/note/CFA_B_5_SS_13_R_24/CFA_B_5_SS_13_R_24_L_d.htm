Commodities Investment Characteristics

 

 

1.       Low correlation to stock and bond.

2.       Business cycle In-sensitive

3.       Certain commodities have positive correlation with inflation (Slightly improve portfolio return during inflation)

 

 

Commodities Investment Approach

 

 

 

 

Basics

 

Certain commodities rise in price during the periods of financial distress and some commodities have positive correlation with inflation (price falls during financial distress). This is because commodity future price is the function of two different factors Convenience Yield (Non-monitory benefits of holding the asset) and Storage Cost, which work in opposite direction in future price determination.

 

 

Future Price (F0) = Spot Price(P0) *e(r+U-Y)T

 

Based on Cost and Carry Model: -

r = Risk free rate

U=Storage cost

Y=Convenience Yield- It's like an embedded consumption-time option in holding a commodity. Commodities, which are difficult to store tend to have high convenience yield.

 

"Storable commodities (like precious metals) future prices are positively correlated with unexpected inflation, certain commodity like (like live stocks), which are difficult to store (have higher convenience yield), which tend to have negative correlation with unexpected inflation (as stock and bond have)". Based on GSCI Index return period 1990-2004.

 

 

Derivate Based Investment (Direct)

 

Commodity Future Return = Spot Return + Collateral Return+ Roll Return

 

Spot Return

Change in spot price over the period of contract (St-So).  As per cost of carry model. It's positive for the short side, if short price go up because short own the commodity. However if future price are locked by the long side so it's will be positive for the long side.

 

Collateral return

The risk-free rate of return you earn because a futures contract does not require payment until the expiration of the contract so that amount can be invested at risk free rate. Higher interest rate will result in higher collateral return and lower Interest rate will result in lower collateral return.

 

Roll return

The return that arises from rolling a futures position forward at expiration.

Roll return = (Change in futures price – Change in spot price)

Change in future = (F(t+1) - F(t))/F(t)

Change in spot = (S(t+1)-S(t))/S(t)

 

 

Roll Return can be either negative and positive depending on term structure of commodity prices and side of the trade you are in.
