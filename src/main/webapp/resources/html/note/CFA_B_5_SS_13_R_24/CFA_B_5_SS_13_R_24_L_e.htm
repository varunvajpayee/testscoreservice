Hedge Fund Characteristics

 

1.       Charged to large management fees.

2.       Generally leveraged.

3.       Generally constituted to take on market in-efficiency (taking long and short).

4.       Generally structured to avoid regulation.

5.       In general, we classify hedge funds as per their strategy.

6.       Fee structure is like private equity (Base AUM and Performance fee (2/20) based on High water mark(HTM).

7.       Generally, have lock up period, (investment cannot be liquidated during this period).

Issues with Hedge with benchmark selection

 

1.      Relevance: Past returns are less relevant because hedge fund indices keeps changing the composition.

2.      Popularity bias- Certain popular fund in indices may receive the greater capital inflow but indices cannot be rebalanced to reflect that.

3.      Survivorship bias- Indices may drop poor performing funds.

4.      Stale Price bias- Funds trading in illiquid market may suffer from this bias.

5.      Backfill bias- Managers who benefit from the missing data have an incentive to supply the past data.

 

 

 

Issues with the sharp ratio as return measure of hedge funds

 

1.       Time Dependency -For hedge funds, monthly/quarterly returns are compounded into annual return.

 

Hedge fund sharp ratio=         (Monthly Return-RFR) *12

Since numerator multiplier is greater than denominator multiplier so sharp ration is biased upward.

 

2.       Assumes Normality- Hedge returns are not normally distributed (negatively skewed).

3.       Assumes liquidity- Hedge fund returns are illiquid. (Downward biased standard deviation so upward biased sharp ration).

4.       Assumes uncorrelated return- Returns are correlated across time will artificially lower the standard deviation.

5.       It uses standalone risk measure (standard deviation) that does not factor in diversification.

 

 

 

Five Issues in hedge performance evaluation.

 

1.       Some hedge funds are absolute return chasers and not a single benchmark can be used to compare their performance due to style drift

2.       CONVENTIONS

a. Funds with higher longer lock up period tend to produce higher return than those with shorter period.

b. Younger funds tend to perform outperform the older funds.

c. Large funds underperform small funds.

3.       Returns

a. Monthly returns are compounded into annual returns.

b. Smooth out the variability investor often compute rolling returns (moving average of 12 months) which undermines the standard deviation.

4.       Leverage

a. Hedge fund return are due to high leverage so deleveraging should be applied to find out the actual returns.

5.       Risk

a. Since hedge returns are negatively skewed so standard deviation fails to measure the true risk of the distribution so sharp ration is not a good measure the hedge fund performance.

b. Downside deviation measures like semi-variance are appropriate for hedge funds, which measure returns below a certain level.

 

 

 

Points

 

·         Implied hurdle rate- It's the minimum expected rate of return which investors should require for a hedge fund allocation.

·         The addition of hedge funds to a portfolio may reduce the total portfolio volatility because there is a weak correlation between hedge fund and other assets in the portfolio. Therefore, hedge funds need only to produce relatively small excess returns over cash to enhance the portfolio risk adjusted return.

·         Global leveraged fund is relatively less leveraged.

·         Rolling returns can provide an understanding into the consistency and cyclicality of hedge fund returns.

·         Generally, lock up periods are under 3 years.
