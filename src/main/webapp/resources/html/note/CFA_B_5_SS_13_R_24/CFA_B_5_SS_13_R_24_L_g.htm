Distress Securities Investing

 

 It involves, buying high risk assets that are in or near bankruptcy at very attractive price and sell them if situation improves.

 

Distress Securities Unique Risk Factors

 

1.        Event Risk: It is known as idiosyncratic risk or firm specific risk. Return from investment is related to the event of the specific company (uncorrelated with the market).

2.       Low Liquidity Risk: Due to cyclic demand, risk of not able to sell.

3.       Market Risk:  Due to macro-economic factors.

4.       J-Factor Risk: Sometimes investment outcome is dependent on court's judge ruling. It’s high for assets in bankruptcy proceedings.

 

Distress Securities investing strategies

 

1.       Long only value investing- It's buying publicly traded below investment grade debt.

2.       Distress debt arbitrage-Purchase company's distress debt while shorting company's equity.

a.       If situation deteriorates- return from shorting equity should be more than debts.

b.       If situation improves- return from bond should be more than equities.

3.       Private Equity

 

Points

·         In the event of a sale at a favorable price, convertible preferred stock typically converts to common equity and is therefore equal in value.

·         If there is a bankruptcy reorg, bondholder do not necessarily have absolute priority over stockholder.

·         When the economy is in a downturn, bankruptcies increase and a distressed securities investment strategy perform WELL even though there is an increase supply of distress securities.

·         When a hedge fund manager engages in distress debt arbitrage (long on debt and short on stocks) and the company's outlook gets worse; company's debt should decrease in value and stock should even DECREASE MORE.

·         LBO distress debt provides an opportunity for debt investors to BUY the distressed debt and then eliminate the prior private equity shareholders in a bankruptcy reorganization plan and become the new private equity shareholders.
