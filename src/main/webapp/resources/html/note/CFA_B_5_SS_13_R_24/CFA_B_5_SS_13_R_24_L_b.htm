REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust)-Indirect

·         Trusts shares are issued on exchange so they tend to have high correlation with equities.

·         They invest in pool of properties and manage those properties.

·         Income from properties is paid as dividends to shareholders

 

REITs to maintain their tax-exempt status

·         90% of profits are paid as dividends.

·         95% of investments in real-estate.

 

REIF (Real Estate Investment Fiduciaries)-Direct

·         REIF returns are specific to property.

·         High transaction cost.

·         Cost of obtaining info is also high.

NCREIT Index

·         It's a principal benchmark for INDIRECT investment.

·         It's a cap weighted index and includes all REITs traded on the NYSE or AMEX.

·         95% stocks in NAREIT index are stocks of equity REIT. 

·         Index returns are calculated after deduction of fees.

NCREIF Index

·         It's a principal benchmark for DIRECT investments.

·         It's a value weighted index for commercially owned properties

·         Because of SMOOTING, reported volatility of real estate returns and correlation with other asserts are LOW, which means the apparent diversification benefits are OVERSTATED.

 (There are statistical methods to correct the smoothing effect).

·         Index returns are calculated before deduction of fees.
