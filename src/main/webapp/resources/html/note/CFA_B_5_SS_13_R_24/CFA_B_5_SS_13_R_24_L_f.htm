Managed Futures Types

 

Managed Futures trading strategies:

a.       Systematic trading (like technical analysis)-It typically apply sets of rules to trade according to short, intermediate and long trends. They may also trade counter to trend in a contrarian strategy.

 

a.        Discretionary trading - The strategy is based on the discretion of the CTA (Community Trading Advisor), in the same way that any active manager seek value.

 

Managed futures heavily use derivative instruments and can also be classified as

 

·         Financial - Uses financial future, forward contract.

·         Currency _ currency future and forward contract.

·         Diversified-financial derivatives, currency derivatives and commodity derivatives.

 

 

Points

 

·         Managed fund is also limited partnership and compensation structure like hedge fund.

 

·         MF has negative correlation with stocks and Bonds when their returns are negative and Positive correlation with their stock and binds return are positive. So, it's partially offset losses in bad economic environment and add to gain in good economic environment.

 

·         Managed futures programs trying to earn a rate of return above the risk-free rate generally helped by the participation of hedger in future market. Hedgers are just looking for protection so they are not willing to earn any excess return.

 

·         Hedgers may pay a risk premium to liquidity providers for the insurance they obtain so managed futures may be able to earn positive excess returns. Differential carrying costs among investors may permit managed fund traders to take advantage of short-term pricing differences between theoretically identical stock, bond, futures, options, and cash market positions.
