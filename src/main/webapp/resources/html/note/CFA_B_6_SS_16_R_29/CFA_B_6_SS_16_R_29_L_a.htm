A) PORTFOLIO EXECUTION

 

1.     Order Types

Market order:

An order to execute the trade immediately at the best possible price.

 

Advantage – speed of execution & certainty in transaction

Disadvantage – price uncertainty 

Limit order:

An order to execute a trade at a limit price or better.

Advantage – price certainty

Disadvantage – execution uncertainty

 

2.     Market Quality

a.       Liquidity (the ability to trade without delay at relatively low cost and in relatively large quantities).

                                       i.            Small Bid ask spread.

                                     ii.            Market depth (Executing Large order without affecting the security price).

                                   iii.            Resilience (Asset prices are close to their intrinsic values).

 

b.       Transparency (Pre-and Post-trade information).

c.       Assurance of trade completion (trades settle without problems under all market conditions).

 

 Liquid market:

§  Many buyers and sellers.

§  Investor characteristics are diverse.

§  A convenient location or trading platform.

§  Integrity as reflected in its participants and platform.

 

3.     The Effective Spread

o    It's used to analyze trade execution. If effective spread is less than market bid-ask spread then trade is executing cheaper than the market.

o    It is always a positive number.

 

Buy order spread = 2*[execution price – mid quote]

Sell order spread = 2*[mid quote - execution price]

 

                                       i.            Price improvement – a trade is executed at better than the bid-asked quote.

                                     ii.            Price impact – a trade is done at worse than the bid-ask quote.

 

4.     Market Structures: 

a.      Quote driven (Dealer Market)

Traders trade with the dealer.

                                       i.            dealer provide guaranteed execution.

                                     ii.            providing liquidity.  

 

b.      Order driven

Traders trade with each other. For example - ECN and automated auction.

                                       i.            No intermediary dealers.

                                     ii.            Lower transaction cost.

                                   iii.            Price discovery based on demand and supply.

                                   iv.            No guarantee execution.

 

c.       Electronic Crossing Network (ECN)

It's a type of Order driven market.

                                       i.            The typical trader is an institution.

                                     ii.            Orders are batched together and crossed at fixed points.

                                   iii.            Low trading costs.

                                   iv.            Anonymity.

                                     v.            No price discovery because of the anonymity. 

 

d.      Broker- Dealer Market

                                       i.      Brokers act in behalf of client (trader) to find the best execution of the trade.

                                     ii.      Broker in exchange of commission, finds the counterparty, represent the trader, and provide secrecy (anonymity) to the trader.

                                   iii.      Dealers are the counterpart, who provides the liquidity in market.
