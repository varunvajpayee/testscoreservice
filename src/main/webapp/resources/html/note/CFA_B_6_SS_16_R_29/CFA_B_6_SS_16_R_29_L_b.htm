B) EXECUTION COSTS

 

Cost associated with a trade can be identified as explicit cost or implicit cost. Explicitly costs are easy to identify like (Taxes, Commissions) whereas implicitly cost are harder to discern like (market impact or Bid-ask spread).

 

1.     Volume Weighted Average Price

 A weighted Average of execution prices during a day. VWAP is also served as a benchmark for traders.

Advantages:

a.       Easy to understand & simple.

b.       Appropriate for comparing small trades in non-trending market.

Limitations:

a.       Can be gamed (trader simply waits until late in the day and decide which trades to execute).

b.       Inappropriate for large volumes and in trending market.

c.       Does not consider missed trades.

 

2.     Implementation shortfall

It is the difference between the return of actual portfolio and the return paper portfolio in which securities prices are recorded at the time of decision (no impact on prices due to delay).

 

BP or DP=Decision Price (Benchmark Price)

Revised Benchmark Price(RBP)- Market price of the security if the order of the security isn't executed on timely fashion.

CP= Cancellation price

EP= execution Price

 

·         Explicit cost – commissions, taxes and fees.

·         Market Impact or Realized profit/loss – (The execution price – the decision price) for the trades executed.  It reflects the price movement from the decision price (when decision taken) to the execution price for the part of the trade executed on the day it is placed.

[EP-DP] *shares Executed

 

·         Delay cost (slippage) – the change in price over the day an order is placed when the order is not executed that day.

[RBP - DP] *shares Executed

·         Missed trade opportunity cost (unrealized profit/loss – The difference between the cancellation price and the decision price, based on the amount of the order not filled.

[CP-DP] *shares Cancelled

Advantages:

a.       Not subject to gaming

b.       Demonstrate the tradeoff between quick execution and market impact.

c.       Decompose and identifies cost

Limitations:

a.       Can be gamed (trader simply waits until late in the day and decide which trades to execute)

b.       Inappropriate for large volumes and in trending market

c.       Does not consider missed trades.
