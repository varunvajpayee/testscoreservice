C) ALGO TRADING AND TRADING TACTICS

 

 

1.     Trader Types

 

a.       Information motivated traders: time sensitive thus prefer quick trades.

b.       Value motivated traders: patient, focus is on price, not on speed (will use limit orders)

c.       Liquidity motivated traders: time sensitive, thus prefer quick trades. They utilize market orders and trades on crossing network and ECN. 

d.       Passive traders: price sensitive, favor limit order.

 

2.     Trading Tactics

a.       Liquidity-at-any-cost - Often used to execute large block of shares quickly. Difficult to keep control over cost and maintaining anonymity.

b.      Costs-are-not-important - Assuming market is efficient so cost to execute trades are not very high (hence not very relevant)

c.       Need-trustworthy-agent - Broker works as a trustworthy agent of trader. He is responsible to do best execution of price and maintain anonymity of trader. It's generally used for thinly traded security.

d.      Advertise-to-draw liquidity. For Example, IPO. It involves cost like marketing cost and administrative cost.

e.       Low-cost-whatever-the-liquidity- Low cost may result into an uncertainty of trade execution.

Logical Participation Strategies(LPS)

 Break Large order into pieces to minimize the market impact. It can be categorized into following categories: -

 

1.       TWAP (Time Weighted average price strategy) spread the trades evenly over time (usually a day). It's used for thinly traded stock that has volatile trading volume.

2.       Percentage of Volume - Percentage of trades are executed in a day until the entire order is fulfilled.

3.       VWAP- already discussed.

 

Specialized Strategies(SS)

Like Smart routing, whereby algorithms are used to intelligently route an order to the most liquid venue.

 

 

Points

 

1.       Crossing networks maintain the complete confidentiality not only regarding the size of the orders and the names of the investors placing the orders, but also regarding the unmatched quantities.

2.       By definition, orders executed on crossing networks, such as POSIT, avoid market impact costs because the orders are crossed at the existing market price determined elsewhere, regardless of size

3.       Directly trading liquid basket has less market impacted then trading these shares individually.

4.       For momentum markets (technical analysis), limit orders are not good. Because limit orders may never be fulfilled if momentum goes in the opposite direction.

5.       Principal trade is a trade with a broker in which the broker commits capital to facilitate the prompt execution of an order that is usually larger and/or more urgent than can be accommodated within the normal level of trading.

6.       Algo trading represents a small shared in NYSE and NASDAQ trading but it's increasing.
