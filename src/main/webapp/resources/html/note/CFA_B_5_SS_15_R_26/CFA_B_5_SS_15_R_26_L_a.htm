5.     Hedging is NOT perfect

 

1.       Round problem

2.       Basis Risk- Item hedged is not perfect match for the hedging vehicle.

3.       Beta and duration are not accurate, keep changing as time passes.

4.       The future and sport price are not priced based on the cash and carry arbitrage model.

 

6.     Exchange Rate Risk

 

1.       Transaction Exposure- Cash flow in foreign currency to be received or paid at the future. This can be hedged using forward contract.

2.       Economic Exposure- Change in currency affect the comparativeness of the company. Yen appreciate is affecting Toyota.

3.       Translation Exposure- Converting foreign denominated financial statements to domestic currency (all current rate and temporal methods).

 

7.     Hedging Market and Currency Risk

 

Foreign portfolio managers can manage both market and currency risk by

 

a.       Selling the forward contract on the foreign market index to hedge market risk.

b.       Selling the forward contract on the foreign currency to hedge the currency risk.

 

They have Four choices.

 

1.       Hedge both

2.       Hedge market not hedge currency

3.       Hedge currency not hedge market

4.       Accept both risk

 

Manager can decide how much to hedge

 

1.       All foreign currency risk

2.       No hedging

3.       Partial hedge (50%) preferred.
