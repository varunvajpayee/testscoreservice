A) LEVERAGE EFFECT

 

 

 

1.     Leverage effect on portfolio return

 

a.       Leverage increases the return of the portfolio relative to unleveraged portfolio provided rate of return is greater than cost borrowing.

b.       As leverage increases, risk (potential losses) also increases.

 

 

Rp = Ru+[(BF/EQ) *(Ru - Rbf]

 

Rp = return on portfolio

Ru = return on unleveraged portfolio

BF = Borrowed Fund

EQ = Equity

Rbf = rate of borrowed funds (cost)

3.     Repurchase Agreements (Repo)

 

·         A repurchase agreement (Repo) is a contract involving the sale of securities to a buyer, with an agreement to repurchase the same securities on a later date.

·         Repo is technically a sale, can also be termed as a collateralized loan.

·         Generally, term of repo is day or two but they can be rolled over.

 

Repo rates will reflect the delivery requirement, (Credit risk decreases from top to bottom) .

 

1.       Highest for no delivery. 

2.       Next for delivery to a custodial account.

3.       Next lowest for wire transfer of title.

4.       Lowest rates provided for physical delivery.
