2.     Hedging Issues

 

A.      Price risk

An unhedged position is exposed to price risk, the risk that the cash market price will move adversely.

 

B.      Basis risk

 

The outcome of a hedge will depend on the relationship between the cash price and the futures price of offsetting investment used in hedging during hedging. This difference between the cash price and the futures price is called the basis. This basis may change in an unpredictable way is called basis risk.

 

C.      Cross Hedge

If no future contract exists for a given position, then cross hedge is used. In cross hedge, Security used in hedging is different from the asset being hedged. (For Example - Treasury bonds are used as underlying to hedge corporate bond's exposure).

 

In hedging, Risk factors exposures to the investment or to the future position (hedging position) should be evaluated periodically. Future position should be used to effectively hedge the position.
