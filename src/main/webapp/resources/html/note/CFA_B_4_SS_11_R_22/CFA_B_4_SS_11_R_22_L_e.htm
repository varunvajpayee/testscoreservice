2.     International Bond Duration

 

Since duration based analysis is good for domestic only bond portfolio, but in international context, foreign bond's rates changes can be different from domestic bond's rates changes.  Yield beta measures the relationship between foreign bond's rate relative to change in domestic bond's rate.

 

Δ rate 𝑓𝑜𝑟𝑒𝑖𝑔𝑛= 𝛽 (Δrate 𝑑𝑜𝑚𝑒𝑠𝑡𝑖𝑐) +error

 

Bond manager wants to understand the impact on foreign bond's prices due to change in the domestic interest rate. He can use following formula: -

 

%Δ Price of foreign Bond = Duration of foreign bond * (𝛽*Δrate 𝑑𝑜𝑚𝑒𝑠𝑡𝑖𝑐)

%Δ Price of foreign Bond = Duration of foreign bond *(Δ rate 𝑓𝑜𝑟𝑒𝑖𝑔𝑛) - Based on above equation

 

Duration contribution for a foreign bond to portfolio = Weight (Foreign Bond's weight in portfolio) *Standardized duration of foreign bond 

Standardized duration of foreign bond = 𝛽* Duration of foreign bond

 

3.     Breakeven Spread Analysis

Breakeven analysis can be used to make relative value and currency hedging decisions between foreign bond markets.   It can be used to quantify the amount of spread widening required to wipe out a foreign yield advantage.  In other words, it’s the determination of how much spreads would have to widen to make the total returns of two different bonds equal.

Change in price = -Duration*breakeven yield change

breakeven yield change =        Change in price

                      -Duration

 

To solve breakeven spread analysis problem, we must divide the yield advantage by the number of periods asked in the question. For example, an annual yield advantage is 100 bps and to convert it into quarter yield advantage, we must divide it by 4 (100/4=25bps).
