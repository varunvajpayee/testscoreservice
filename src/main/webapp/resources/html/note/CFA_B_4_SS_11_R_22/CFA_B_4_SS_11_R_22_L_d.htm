D) USE OF DERIVATIVES TO MANAGE DURATION

 

1.     Interest rate swap

 

As we know, IR Swap can be used to convert fixed rate loan (bond) to floating rate loan (bond) or vice versa. IR Swap does impact the portfolio (bond) duration also.

Receiving fixed rate payments = Increases the duration of portfolio

Paying fixed rate payments = Decreases the duration of portfolio

Floating rate bonds have little impact on duration because market value is reset at every coupon payment.

 

Example: -

Dollar duration of a swap (floating rate payer or fixed rate receiver) =Dollar duration of a fixed-rate bond–Dollar duration of a floating.

 

2.     Interest Rate Options

The duration of an option depends on

 

·          Duration of the underlying.

·          Option Delta - (Change in price of option relative to change in price of underlying).

·          Leverage - (Price of underlying relative to price of option)

 

Duration of Option = Duration of Underlying * Option Delta * Leverage

 

Where

 

Leverage = Price of underlying

Price of option

 

Option Delta =        Change in price of option

Change in price of underlying

 

 

3.     Types of Credit Derivatives

 

The buyer uses credit derivatives to transfer the credit risk associated with the underlying security.

 

1.      Credit Options

Credit (binary option) put option

they are written on bond's price.  Option Value=max[(strike-value),0]; Generally, their payoffs is based on the occurrence of a specified negative credit event (Like downgrade).

 

Credit Spread Call Options

Pay off if bond's spread goes above the spread specified in the option contract.

Max [(Spread at the option maturity – Option contract spread) × Notional amount × Risk factor,0]

 

Risk factor is the interest sensitivity of the debt and it represents the dollar value of 1bps in the credit spread. That's related to dollar duration and convexity of the bond.

 

2.      Credit Forwards

Like credit spread call options, the buyer of a credit forward contract benefits from a widening credit spread and the seller benefits from a narrowing credit spread.

 

Payoff = (Credit spread at maturity – Contract spread) × Notional amount × Risk factor

 

 

3.      CDS

It's an agreement that the seller of the CDS will compensate the buyer (Buyer of the bond) in the event of a loan default (seller the debtor) or other credit event.
