C) INVESTMENT POLICY STATEMENT PROCESS

 

A) Benefits of an IPS

 

1.       Facilitates a thorough review and ensure investment continuity.

2.       An IPS is portable and easy to understand

3.       Produce realistic investment goals (and serve as discipline)

4.       A Document of understand, which protects both an advisor and the client.

 

 

B) Constructing an IPS

 

1.     Return

 

a.       Return objectives are attainable objectives after considering capital market expectations and client's constraints.

b.       Required return to meet an investor’s long term financial objective.

c.       Desired return secondary (non-primary) objectives.

d.       Alternative to divide income into income and growth components. A “total return” approach looks first at the individual’s investment goals and then identifies the annual after-tax portfolio return necessary to meet those goals.

e.       Personal residence should not be treated as part of investable asset.

 

After tax nominal return = (Real return before tax + Inflation) *(1 - Effective tax rate)

 

2.     Risk

 

A.      Ability to Take Risk It depends on the following factors.

 

Timeframe- Longer time horizon more risk can be taken.

Importance- Critical goals (Living expense) indicates lower ability to take risk.

Flexibility - if Investor's goals and amount are flexible and can be easily changed then Investor has greater ability to take risk.

Wealth- More Wealth, more risk can be taken.

 

B.      Willingness to take risk - Situational profiling helps in assessing an individual’s willingness to take risk. Generally active investors have greater willingness to take risk than passive investors.

 

 

3.     Liquidity (Constraint)

·         Includes ongoing expenses, emergency reserves, and negative liquidity events (Home Improvement).

·         The higher the liquidity need, the less the portfolio’s ability to take risk

·         Liquidity in security context-More liquid a security is, it's easier to get out of that security by converting it into cash. It will also be less costly to convert it into cash because of less transaction cost.

·         When calculating required return if a cash reserve is specified in advance. This cash reserve should not be included in investable assets.

 

 

4.     Time Horizon

 

·         The longer time horizon, the higher risk tolerance, because there is more time to make up for loss. 

·         Short term (less than 3 years), Intermediate (3-15 years), and long term (over 15).

·         Generally, time horizon is divided into number of stages. Most individual have multiple stages. However elderly investor with limited financial resource might have a single stage time horizon.

 

  5)  Taxes

·         An entire reading is based on taxes. There are four broad categories of taxes: -

 

1.       Income tax

2.       Capital Gains tax

3.       Wealth transfer tax

4.       Property tax

 

·         There are Tax deferral and avoidance strategies like investing in tax deferral accounts like (401k, IRS, Roth IRS) or loss harvesting.

 

 

6.     Legal and regulatory environment

 

An entire section is based on this topic.

·         This is country and client specific. Most frequently involved taxation and transfer of ownership.

·         Issues related to "personal trust (revocable and irrevocable) are discussed here.

 

7.     Unique circumstances

 

·         Catch all bucket to place any unique constraints.

·         Guidelines for social investing and trading restrictions.

·         Forbidden asset classes due to SRI Investing.

·         Concentrated position.

·         Bequest preference.

 

B) Strategic Asset Allocation

 

 The idea is to have strategic asset allocation with in the portfolio so that investor's return and risk expectation can be met. We will discuss it more in the following readings.  An entire reading is dedicated to this.

 

Monte Carlo Simulation

it can be used to determine the asset allocation. It has following advantages.

 

Advantages

 

1.       Considers path dependency and provides probability of any given outcome.

2.       Complex scenarios, which includes tax modeling analysis, after tax income and tax differed income, can be made.

3.       Clear understanding of tradeoff between return and risk.

4.       It's easier to assess the multi period effect.

 

Disadvantages

 

1.       Since historical inputs are used in the model so predicted future outcome may not accurate.

2.       It's very computation intensive.  Computers with large processing powers are needed to run the scenarios and aggregate the results.

3.       After considering the impact of portfolio's fee and taxes, portfolio return may not match with model's output return.
