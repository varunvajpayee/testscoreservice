B) SITUATIONAL PROFILING

 

A) Situational profiling includes assessing: -

 

1.      Source of wealth (Active vs passive- Barnewell 2-way model)

 

Active Investors: They usually risked their own capital to gain wealth (Like Entrepreneurs).  Generally, they have a higher tolerance for risk than they have need for security. Generally, they are more comfortable, in situation where they feel in control.

 

Passive Investors: They have not risked their own capital to gain wealth but got wealthy passively. They may not know much about investing (risking capital) . Most like they are long term savers or inheritors who save money during their jobs. Due to low risk tolerance, passive investors prefer to have a diversified portfolio.

 

 

2.      Measure of wealth (subjective nature of financial well-being)

 

Investors who perceive their wealth as small may demonstrate lower tolerance for portfolio volatility than investors who perceive their holdings as large. An investor, who is concerned with succession/estate planning, perceive portfolio to be large hence are the risk taker. A positive correlation exists between the perception of portfolio size and the willingness to take risk

 

3.      Stage of life

Four main stages of life and investment policy depends on these stages: -

 

a.      Foundation

The individual establishes the foundation based on that wealth will be accrued. Foundation could be a establishment of a business, or the acquisition of educational degrees. Asset base is low and greatest ability to take risk because of long time horizon.

 

b.      Accumulation

Wealth is accumulated based on skills acquired in foundation phase. Both income and expenses rise in this phase. Investable asset base increases.

 

c.       Maintenance (Retirement phase)

Retirement focuses on maintaining the desired lifestyle and financial security. Emphasis shifts to preserving wealth and risk tolerance is begin to decline.

 

d.      Distribution

Wealth is transferred to other persons in this phase or also post death in form of bequests. Estate planning and dealing with tax constrains become important in this phase.

 

 

B) Investor personality types

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

                                       Decisions (thinking)                  Decisions (feeling)

 

More Risk Averse       Methodical                                    Cautious 

 

Less Risk Averse          Individualist                                  Spontaneous   

 

 

 

a.      Methodical

Relies on hard facts by following market analysts and do research.

b.      Individualist

Exhibit investment independence (self-assured approach)

c.       Cautious

Generally averse to potential losses.  Desire low volatility investments (Not a decision Maker). They Generally have low portfolio turnover.

d.      Spontaneous

Constantly readjust portfolio which causes high turnover(cost).  Concerned with missing a trend.
