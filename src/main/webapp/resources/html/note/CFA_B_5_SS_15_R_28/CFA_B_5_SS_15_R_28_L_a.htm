A) RISK MANAGEMENT APPLICATIONS OF SWAP STRATEGIES

 

A.    Interest Rate Swap

 

IRS is used to convert a floating-rate loan to a fixed-rate loan (or fixed rate loan to floating rate loan). As shown in below picture, 

 

Party A has current obligation to pay floating rate loan of (LIBOR + 1.50%). Party A can convert it into a fixed rate loan by participating in a swap agreement with party B, where Party A would pay fixed rate of (8.65%) in exchange, A would receive (LIBOR + 0.70%) floating rate of interest in return, which he can use to pay off his obligation. Total Amount A would pay = (LIBOR+0.7%) - 8.65%  - (LIBOR+1.5%) = 9.45%.

 

 

Picture Source :https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Vanilla_interest_rate_swap.png

 

Swap duration

 

Duration is a measure of sensitivity to interest rates. Change in interest rate would have higher impact on fixed rate instrument because present value of cash flows would change.

 

For floating rate bonds, coupon gets reset at every settlement date (usually six months) so their duration is near to zero. Bonds with quarterly payment will have duration = 0.25 for floaring rate bond (In case of ferbosi method, this value is further divided by 2, 0.25/2 =0.125). Apply ferbosi method, only if a question explicitly asks for it otherwise for bond with quarterly floating rate payment would have 0.25 duration.

 

Swap duration is the duration of what you receive minus duration of what you pay. So, Fixed rate receiver would have higher duration than floating rate receiver.

 

Net Duration = Duration of which you receive - Duration which you pay.

 

Swap to change duration

 

Adding swaps to your current portfolio, you can change the duration of the portfolio. (like future contract). If you want to increase the duration of the portfolio then you want to receive fixed or if you want to decrease the portfolio duration then you want to pay fixed rate in the swap.

 

Target portfolio*Dt= current portfolio*Dp+Swap notional principal*Swap duration

 

Dt = Target Duration

Dp = Portfolio Duration

 

Cash Flow Risk

With Floating rate bond, you don't know the amount of the cash as interest rate keeps on changing so floating rate bond are subject to the cash flow risk. Since floating rate bonds PV stay almost the same because cash flow keeps changing as interest rate changes so they don't have much INTEREST RATE RISK.

 

Interest rate risk(Duration)

With Fixed rate bond, cash flows are predefined so they don't have cash flow risk but their present value keeps changing with the interest rate so they have interest rate risk(Duration).

 

B.     Currency Swap

As shown in the following picture. Currently swap involves series of steps.  Here is a simplified example.

 

Step 1) US company borrows $30,000,000 from a US bank and give to UK company in a swap transaction. In return, UK company Borrows £ 20,000,000 from a UK bank and give to US company as part of Swap transaction.  (Initial principals are exchanged).

 

Step 2) Assuming interest payments are made on semi-annually basis. 

Semiannually US Co. pays to UK Co. = £20,000,000 * 9/2= £ 900,000

Semiannually UK Co. pays to US Co. = $30,000,000 * 8/2= £ 1,200,000

 

 Step 3) Principal Re-Exchange at the end. US Company pays back £20,000,000 to UK company. UK company pays back $30,000,000 to US company and they can settle their obligations with their respective banks.

 

 

 

 

Currency risk in swaps in only the interest payment not in principal amount. Since same principal paid back to the original company.

 

 

C.     Equity Swap

Counterparties exchange their trading returns as part of Equity Swaps. Generally, in equity swap, portfolio's return are exchanged with Index returns. Equity swap involves basis risk as stock portfolio return doesn't exactly match with Index return (which involved in the swap).

 

Benefits of using swap

 

1.       Transaction cost for the equity swap are generally lower than transaction cost to gain exposure by buying selling stocks.

2.       Easy to diversify the portfolio using equity swaps.

 

4.     Swaption

 

It's a type of a European option written on SWAP. In payer swaption, Fixed rate payer side exercise it in future if SWAP rate on the exercise day is higher than the agreement SWAP rate. Swaption can also be used to terminate an existing swap.
