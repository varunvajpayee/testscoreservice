TRADITIONAL AND BEHAVIORAL FINANCE

 

Traditional finance

 

1.       People are Risk Averse.

2.       Based on rational Expectation (REM= Rational Economic Man).

3.       Market is efficient, where prices reflect all available information. All investors are assumed to have same information and interpret it accurately and instantly.

4.       Portfolio are constructed using theorems like CAMP (considering correlation among assets).

5.       Traditional finance is normative (Indicating how people should make decisions). 

 

Utility theory

 

In traditional finance

 

1.       Individual (REM) always look to maximize the utility (derived satisfaction from economic decision).

2.       Utility assumes diminishing marginal rate of return for example (Individuals need more money for each additional unit of the work) so people are risk averse.

 

 

 

In Behavioral finance approach of Utility Theory (Counter argument against "people are risk averse")

 

1.       People can be risk averse, neutral or risk seeker depending on their level of wealth.

2.       Risk Neutral: Person have same utility for gains or losses.

3.       Risk Averse: Concave shape, suffers a greater loss of utility for a given loss than the same amount of gain. It also indicates diminishing marginal utility, as over all wealth increases, utility begins to  increase at slower rate.

4.       Risk Seeking: Convex shape, for each additional unit of wealth provides more utility.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Efficient Market Hypothesis

 

In traditional finance

·         Market is efficient, where prices reflect all available information.

·         All investors are assumed to have same information and interpret it accurately and instantly.

 

 

 

Forms of market efficiency

a.       Weak Market efficiency = current prices incorporate all past price and volume info. We cannot generate excess return using technical analysis.

b.       Semi-strong market efficiency= All public information including past price and volume info. We cannot generate excess return using technical and fundamental analysis.

c.       String market efficient= All information (including non-public-insider) information is incorporated in prices. No analysis can generate excess return.

 

Behavioral finance approach of Efficient Market (Counter argument against "Market is efficient")

 

A.      Fundamental Anomalies: A fundamental anomaly is an irregularity that emerges when one considers a stock’s future performance based on a fundamental assessment (P/E or P/B) of the stock’s value. Examples of fundamental anomalies are the performance of small-capitalization companies and value companies compared to large-capitalization companies and growth companies, respectively. It challenges semi strong market efficiency hypothesis and Strong market hypothesis.

 

B.      Technical Anomalies: Moving Averages based (auto trader) analysis. Challenges all hypothesis including weak, excess return based on buy and sell signals.

 

C.      Calendar anomalies: appears to show that certain small cap stocks have high returns in January, challenges all hypothesis including weak.

 

 

 

Behavioral finance- Bounded Rationality (Counter argument against individuals have same information)

 

1.       It acknowledges the limitation of knowledge capacity and Intellectual of individuals.

2.       IT's not possible to have every individual to have same information about the future outcome.

3.       People tend to satisfice (by accepting satisfactory outcome even though It's not optimal)

 

 

Prospect Theory (Counter argument again Individuals are risk averse)

 

 

1.       People are Loss Averse (Not Risk Averse). Individuals are risk seekers in the domain of losses (convex portion of the curve) while risk-averse in the domain of gains (concave portion of the curve).

2.       Investor care about relative value of change of wealth than the absolute change in wealth.

3.       Loss aversion can lead to selling winners too early and holding losers for longer period. It is also known as “disposition effect”.    

4.       There are two phases of prospect theory.

A) Editing Phase: where prospects are coded, combined, segregated, cancelled, and simplified to reduce the number of choices.

B) Evaluation phase: Investors value their prospects and expected output. they compute a utility function based on the expected output and their respective probabilities and then choose the prospect that has a higher utility.
