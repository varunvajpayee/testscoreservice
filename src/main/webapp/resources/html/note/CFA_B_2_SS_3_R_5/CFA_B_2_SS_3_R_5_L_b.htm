BEHAVIORAL FINANCE MODELS

 

As we saw, approach to portfolio management in tradition finance is not optimal so these four models in behavioral finance try to add client's unique characteristics to traditional portfolio management.

 

1.     Behavioral Approach to Consumption and Savings

 

a.       In traditional Finance, people save money to fund their retirement by exercising self-control on their current consumption. But behavioral finance questions the ability to exercise control. This self-control bias leads to different ways of framing and bucketing current income, currently owned assets, and the present value of future income (mental accounting), where an investor’s marginal propensity to consume is highest for current income.

b.       Investment decisions made when individuals are subject to self-control, mental accounting, and framing biases differ from those of the rational economic individuals of traditional finance.

3.     Behavioral Portfolio Theory

 

Investors construct their portfolios layer by layer as per their returns and risks expectations, which varies between these layers.  In behavioral finance, portfolio construction is the function of following factors:-

 

a.       Importance of goal- If a goal is more important, then the assets selected for the layer are likely to be riskier or more speculative in nature.

b.       Investor’s utility function. (risk averse (concave shape) investor have higher number of assets to reduce the risk).

c.       Information advantage (Concentrated high risk investment due to information advantage).

d.       Loss averse (Keeping liquidity in portfolio more than the requirement).

e.       Other factors like required return to meet the goal.

 

4.     Adaptive Markets Hypothesis

 

It's traditional Efficient Market Hypothesis with blend of behavioral finance concepts like bounded rationality, satisficing and evolutionary principal. Success depends on the ability of an individual to survive rather than to achieve highest expected utility.

 

Implications of the AMH are:

a.       The relationship between risk and reward changes over time (Evolution).

b.       active management can add value by adopting new strategies.

c.       No one strategy will work all the time. (Strategy should be changed like Business Cycle)

d.       Adapting new things and innovation is critical for survival;

e.       survival is the essential objective.
