B) CURRENCY MANAGEMENT

1.     The domestic-currency return on a foreign asset

 

RDC = (1+RFC) (1+RFX) – 1

RDC = RFC + RFX + RFC*RFX

RDC= Return in domestic currency

RFC = Return in foreign currency (Local currency)

 

2.     Volatility Decomposition

σ2(RDC) = σ2(RFC) + σ2(RFX) + 2σ(RFC)(RFX)ρ(RFC, RFX)

4.     Currency Management Strategies

a.       Passive Hedging – rule based approach, currency risk = 0.  Consideration: cost & rebalancing.

b.       Discretionary hedging – some limited discretion on how far to allow actual portfolio exposure.

c.       Active currency management – it is the extension of discretionary hedge.  This should be done within mandated risk limits though to add alpha.

d.       Currency overlay – Active currency management.  Outsourcing currency exposures to a firm specializing in FX management.

5.     Arguments in favor of fully hedged currency exposure are

 

a.       Short term the investment objectives of the portfolio.

b.       Risk averse the beneficial owners of the portfolio are.

c.       Immediate the income and/or liquidity needs of the portfolio.

d.       Fixed income assets are held in a foreign currency portfolio.
