C) TACTICAL CURRENCY MANAGEMENT

 

1.     Economic fundamental

 The basic idea is that currency value falls to its fundamental value. Long term currency is based on purchasing power parity.  Increase in value of currency is associated with

 

a.       Currency is undervalued relative to their fundamental value.

b.       High real interest rate.

c.       Low inflation rate.

d.       Countries with decreased risk premium.

 

2.     Technical analysis

 The basic idea is that Future currency prices are dependent on past performance.

Support level- floor price based on trend.

Resistance level- ceiling price based on trend.

 

Market technicians believe that 

a.       The historical data can be helpful in projecting future price movements

b.       The historical patterns tend to repeat.

c.       Does not attempt to determine where market prices should trade but where they will trade.

3.     Carry Trade

Borrow in a cheaper currency (forward premium currency) and invest in higher interest currency (forward discount currency where F<S). Interest rate parity doesn't hold in real life that's the reason carry trade are successful. Basic Characteristics are: -

 

a.       Are unhedged trade.

b.       Work in a normal market condition (volatility is low).

c.       Violation of unbiased parity theorem.

 

4.     Volatility Trading.

Buy/Sell Straddle or strangle on currencies as volatility(Vega) increases/decreases. Based on the concept of delta hedging.

 

·         Straddle are at ATM put and call, most expensive.  

·         Strangle are OTM (out of the money) puts and calls option and they are cheaper than straddle.
