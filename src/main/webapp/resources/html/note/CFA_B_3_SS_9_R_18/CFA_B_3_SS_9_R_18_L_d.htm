D) TOOLS OF CURRENCY MANAGEMENT

1.     Forward contract vs futures contracts

Forward contracts are favored over futures contracts for the following reasons:

 

·         Future contracts standardized: They are not bespoke instrument. Whereas forward contracts can be made for any currency.

·         Futures contracts are not as popular as forward. So forward contracts are more liquid.

·         Futures contracts require up front margin (initial margin).

2.     Types of currency Hedges

Forward hedge

sell forward contract on the exposed currency to cover up the currency risk.

 

Proxy hedge

Proxy hedge are used when forward contracts are NOT available on exposed currency to cover the currency risk. High correlated currency acts as a proxy for the exposed currency. So, investors sell forward contract on highly correlated currency.

 

Cross Hedge

There are forward currency contracts available on exposed currency. You transfer the currency risk from exposed currency to an another (3rd) currency because you expect to make more money on the 3rd currency. 

3.     Hedge ratios with forward contracts

 

a.       Static hedge: unchanging hedge thus avoid transaction costs, but will also have unwanted currency exposures.

b.       Dynamic hedge: rebalances the portfolio periodically.
