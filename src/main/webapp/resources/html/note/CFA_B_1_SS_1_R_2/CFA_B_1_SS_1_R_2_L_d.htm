IV.                  DUTIES TO EMPLOYERS

 

A.      Loyalty (act for the benefit of your employer)

 

1.You cannot take any property while leaving unless you have a consent from employer.
2. There is no requirement to put employer interests ahead of family and other personal obligations.
3. Member can recreate model for your new employer (from your memory) but MUST recreate the supporting documentation to main compliance with "record retention".
4. Companies internal compliance should also be followed along with law,rules,regulations and CFA Institute code of conducts.
5. when accepting work from 3rd party to work in your free time(in the same field of your employer) then you should take written approval from your employer and also get written approval from the 3rd party and let 3rd party know who your employer is.

 

B.      Additional Compensation Arrangements

1. unless a written consent is obtained from all parties involved.
2. unrelated to your employer field of work and you do part time job in your own time then you don't have to disclose those compensation arrangements to your employer and don’t have to disclose the duration of the contract.

 

C.      Responsibilities of Supervisors

 

1. anyone subject to their supervision or authority complies with applicable laws, rules, regulations, and the Code and Standards.
2. If supervisor is supposed to implement CFA complaint procedure, he should first find out "adequate" procedures, which are designed to meet the Industry standard, regulatory requirements, the requirements of the Code and Standards, and the circumstances of the firm.
3. Responsibility to implement and the authority to enforce the procedures should both reside with the compliance officer. Periodically update the procedures. Failing to do can be treated as violation of IV(C).
