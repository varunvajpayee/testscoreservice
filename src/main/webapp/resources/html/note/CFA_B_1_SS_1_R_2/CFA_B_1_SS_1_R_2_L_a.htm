I.                  PROFESSIONALISM

                              A.            Knowledge of the Law (Including regulations and CFA standards)

 

1. You should know the laws and adhere to most strict law (among the applicable laws).
2. Encounter violation by others, first approach to supervisor and/or compliance Dept., if in action continues then leave the employer in extreme case. Continue association may be construed as knowing participation.

 

B.      Independence and Objectivity (Reasonable care and judgement)

 

1. Issuer paid research fee should NOT be dependent on outcome of the research.
2. Modest Gifts: They can be accepted from both clients and other 3rd parties and they DO NOT need to be disclosed to employer.
3. Non-Modest Gifts: they can be only accepted from client, if they are not impacting the Independence and Objectivity and should be disclosed to employer.
4. Past performance gifts from clients are fine, if they are fully disclosed the employer. 
5. Assume (something greater than $500) can impact your independence and objectivity. High society dinners are expensive and not easy to get in.
6. For Issuer paid research, Bonus compensation arrangement should not be allowed, Flat fee compensation is recommended in this case.

 

C.      Misrepresentation

1. Plagiarism is bad. You can use public citations in your research (i.e. S&P public ratings) without attribution.  You can rely on third party research if it has reasonable and adequate basis and property citation is mandatory. 
2. For third party performance presentation, only citation is mandatory, you don't have to take permission from them explicitly.

3. Guaranteed past performance is misrepresentation. No guarantee can be promised.

4.Best practice would be either to obtain the complete study from its original author and cite only that author or to use the information provided by the intermediary and cite both sources (Intermediary and the author).

5. Performance history and records should not be misrepresented.

 

D.     Misconduct

 

1. must not engage in any professional conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, or deceit (false vague statements) or commit any act that reflects adversely on their professional reputation, integrity, or competence.
2. Acts of civil disobedience in support of personal beliefs are allowed.
3. Misdemeanors like DUI are not considered as violation.
