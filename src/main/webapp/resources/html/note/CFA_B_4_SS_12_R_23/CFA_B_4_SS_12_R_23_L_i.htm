Socially Responsible Investing (SRI)

 

It's also known as ethical investing. It uses social, moral, and ethical concerns in screening the stocks. For example: Some investors avoid investing in alcohol, tobacco and energy stocks. Generally, SRI Investors are biased towards small cap and tech stocks.

 

Points

 

·         Separate or pooled indexed accounts are established mostly for institutional investors, normally are low-cost products (lower than ETF).

 

·         Sell side equity research is mostly done by brokerage firms that use equity research to generate business for themselves.

 

·         Buy side equity research is mostly done by investment firms that use equity research as a means in the ongoing construction of an equity portfolio.

 

·         Managers with Positive information ratios are good. Negative information return means negative active return even though tracking error is minimal.

 

·         Ad valorem fee structures are both simple and predictable. The ad valorem fee structure is calculated by multiplying the value of the assets by fee percentage.

 

·         A market-neutral portfolio carries no systematic risk, therefore has a zero beta, and should be measured against the risk-free rate.

 

·         The portfolio manager is willing to buy both value and growth stocks (regardless of P/E). He focuses solely on whether the stock is trading below its intrinsic value. This approach is also known as a blend or core style regarding equity investing, which is an intermediate grouping for investment disciplines that cannot be clearly categorized as value or growth.
