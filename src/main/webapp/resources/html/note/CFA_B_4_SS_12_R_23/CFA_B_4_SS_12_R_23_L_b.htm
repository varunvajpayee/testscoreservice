B)  EQUITY INDEX WEIGHTING

 

1.     Capitalization weighting/market value weighting/market cap weighting/ cap weighting

 

Market Value weights = Price * Number of shares outstanding (free float= excluding securities which can't be traded)

Examples of US capitalization-weighted indexes include the S&P 500 and the Russell indexes.

 

Advantage:

 

1.       Weighting companies by market value is an objective way of measuring the relative importance.

2.       Macro Consistency -If all investors held all the securities in cap-weighted indexes in proportion to their market value, then all shares would be held, with none left over.

3.       Based on CAMP market portfolio.

4.       Doesn't require rebalancing for stock splits and stock dividends.

 

Disadvantage:

 

1.       Overpriced securities could overly influence these indexes.

The capitalization-weighted index is thus potentially more susceptible to market bubbles

2.       (Less diversified) the index may be overly concentrated because large issues will be weighted the most heavily.

3.       Index will not serve as a valid benchmark for active managers, who are seeking excess return by deviating from benchmark.

 

 

2.     Price weighting

 

The performance of a price-weighted index represents the performance of a portfolio that holds one unit of each index security. Dow jones average is an example.

 

Advantage:

 

1.       Simple/Easy to construct.

2.       Price trend (history) available.

 

Disadvantage:

 

1.       Doesn’t reflect typical portfolio creation. Most portfolios do not buy equal number of securities.

2.       The highest-priced securities overly influence price-weighted indexes.

3.       Stocks that appreciate often experience stock splits, and their weights in the index will decrease. Successful companies will become underrepresented, creating a downward bias in the index return

 

 

 

3.     Equal weighting

 

The performance of an equal-weighted index represents the performance of a portfolio that invests the same amount of wealth in each index security.

 

Advantage:

 

1.       Give smaller weights to large-cap securities (and larger weights to small-cap securities) than indexes formed by cap weighting. By reducing exposures to the largest-cap securities, the index is thereby less concentrated in those securities and more DEVESIFIED.

2.       These indexes may better represent “how the market did” because they provide an average of all index security returns.

 

Disadvantage:

 

1.       Small Business Bias: - More weighted to small cap companies.

2.       Requires frequent rebalancing to maintain equal weights.

3.       Inclusion of small issuers means that investors tracking the index may not be able to find liquidity in some of these issues.

 

 

4.     Fundamental weighting

It uses company financial fundamental indicators like sales, cash flow, book value, and dividends, to weight securities (except market values). These indexes seek to weight securities using fundamental values, rather than the market prices of capitalization and price weighting. They are less affected by market price bubbles.
