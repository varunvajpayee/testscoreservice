E) EQUITY INVESTOR STYLES

 

 

 

 

 

 

1.     Value Style

Focused on paying a relatively low share price in relation to earnings or assets per share.

 

It has three substyles:

 

1.       A low P/E investor -look for low priced stocks (focus on the numerator of P/E, P/B). Such stocks are generally found in industries categorized as defensive, cyclical (Utility/Finance Sector).

 

2.       A contrarian -Generally invest in stocks which have low earnings and low probability to rise.

 

3.       High dividend yield - prospects of increasing the dividend in future.

 

2.     Growth Style

Focused on investing in high-earnings-growth companies. Investors focus on the denominator in P/E, P/B ratios and pay above market multiple for companies with superior growth. They also tend to invest in companies in growth industries, such as technology, health care, and consumer products.

 

The growth style has at least two substyles:

1.       consistent growth

2.       earnings momentum (hold the stock if earning trend continues)

 

3.     Market oriented

 An intermediate grouping for investment disciplines that cannot be clearly categorized as value. These investors fall somewhere between value and growth investors and may adopt different strategies at different times.

 

Market oriented investment style-

 

1.       Growth at a price

2.       Growth biased (tilted)

3.       Value biased (tilted)

4.       Style rotation (Opportunist)
