G) EQUITY INVESTMENT STRATEGIES

 

1.     Long-Short Portfolio

 

  1. This has an opportunity to earn two alphas. One for long only position and another from short position.
  2. Long-short investor can eliminate systematic risk (market neutral strategy) by using a pair trade (long and short stocks in same sector/industry).

 

Equitizing a Market Neutral Long-Short Portfolio (Equitizing alpha)

 

In long-short strategy, Proceedings from short sale can be used to fund the long position and portfolio's available cash can be fully collateralized to get the market exposure by going long on market futures contracts (or a market ETF).  

 

1.       Risk free return on the cash holding.

2.       Market return on the future contracts. (Beta)

3.       Spread earned on the long and short positions. (Two alphas)

 

Pricing inefficiencies on Short side

 

1.       Barriers to short sales (Need to own a share or borrow it before shorting it or Margin Requirements).

2.       Firm promote their stock through accounting manipulation, which makes it difficult to find out undervalued stock.

3.       Due to firms' management pressure, sell side analyst are reluctant to issue sell recommendation.

 

 

Alpha and Beta Separation (Portable alpha)

 

Generally Long-short strategy is a pure alpha strategy and a beta exposure can be gained by investing in inexpensive index fund. In this way, returns from these two components can be separated. By dividing, the total return into alpha and beta components, Investor can better manage his/her return and active risk (which is difficult to do in long only approach). Due to market constraints like (cost of implementing the strategy or true market neutral long/short positions), this strategy is difficult to implement.

 

2.    Short Extension Strategies

 

Instead of creating market neutral long/short strategy. In short extension, investor still maintain the exposure of the market for his benefit. For Example: a 110/10 portfolio will short 10% of the portfolio's market value and use those proceeds to gain 110% of long position.

3.    Sell Disciplines

Set up an upside and downside price sell limit to maximize profit and minimize loss. Example

a.       Down from cost (Sell when a position declines in value)

b.       Up from cost

c.       Valuation level

d.       Target price

 

 

4.    Enhanced Indexing (Semi active strategy)

 

A.    Stock-based enhanced indexing

It starts with an index portfolio and then overweighs or underweights individual stocks based on his opinion. If manager has no expectation about certain stocks, then he leaves the weight as like the benchmark. (However, in active management where manager will not even invest in this case.).

 

 

B.      Synthetic enhanced index strategies

It involves gaining exposure to the benchmark using futures/swap (beta exposure by equitizing cash) while generating alpha by investing typically in fixed income security (based on yield curve expectation).

 

 

Limitation of enhanced indexing: -

 

1.       Model will be copied and their alpha will disappear.

2.       Model obtained from historical data may not be applicable to the future.
