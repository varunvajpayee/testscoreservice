D) EQUITY PORTFOLIO INDEXING

 

1.     Full Replication

a.       Buying all the stocks in the index as per their weights.

b.       It's only possible when there are less number of stocks in the index (Like SnP500).

Advantage

a.       Low tracking error.

Disadvantage

a.       Higher Trading cost.

 

 

2.     Stratified Sampling

Managers divides the stocks of an index into a cell based matrix based on certain stocks fundamentals factors like (Industry size or P/E ratios) and then investing in some representative stocks with in each of these cells.

Advantage

a.       This approach is most useful if the benchmark has many stocks or those stocks are illiquid.

Disadvantage

a.       It doesn't consider the correlation among the different pairs of stocks being considered.

b.       JUDGEMENT is involved to pick the more liquid stocks so that transaction costs can be minimized.

c.       Since stocks are picked on Judgment (not necessary from the index) so it doesn't minimize the tracking error.

 

3.     Optimization

It's Based on a factor model, factors between portfolio and index are the same.

a.       It considers the correlation among the different pairs pf stocks.

b.      It does minimize the tracking error by selecting sample stocks with in the index.

 

Advantage

 

a.       Factor model accounts for the correlation between risk factors whereas stratified sampling assumes risk factors are uncorrelated.

b.       Generally Optimization has lower tracking risk than stratified sampling.

 

Disadvantage

 

a.       Risk sensitivities are based on historical data and they are subject to change.

b.       Optimization may provide misleading model if a security or time skews the sample data.

c.       Frequent rebalancing in the model to update latest risk sensitivities.

 

 

Points

 

1.       Optimization should be used only in situations in which full replication would result in substantial transaction costs.
