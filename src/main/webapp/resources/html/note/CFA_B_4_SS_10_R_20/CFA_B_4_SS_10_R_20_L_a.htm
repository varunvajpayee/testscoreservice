2.     Yield continuum

 

 

 

 

OAS Vs Z spread

 

 

 

 

 

 

Z spread

·         Zero volatility spread's calculation is done by trial and error and zero volatility spread assume no volatility in the market so it can only be used for option free bonds.

·         Z spread (Static spread) reflects credit risk + liquidity risk. If we use it for securities with embedded option, it gives wrong value because it adds option value to the spread.

 

OAS (Option adjusted Spread)

 

·         OAS (Option adjusted Spread) reflects credit risk + liquidity risk + Market risk; OAS= total spread - option value.

·         Since we are assuming option can be exercised based on interest rates path so their value cannot be calculated using trial and error. You need some simulation (Monte Carlo Simulation) to generate various path and calculate average spread based on that.

·         When spread widens, both long and short duration bonds value go down but long duration bond value go down more than short duration bond value. Opposite holds true when spread narrows, Long duration bond value go up more than the short duration bond value.

 

 

3.     Versions of Duration

 

 

 

 

Definition

Duration is the slope (first derivative or sensitivity) of price yield curve. You can use duration to find out the percent price change for a given percentage change in the interest rate.

 

Percentage change in price = -  Duration * Percentage change in interest rate.

 

Convexity

1.       Convexity is the (curvature/second derivative/) of price yield curve.

2.       Duration/Convexity are good in approximating the bond price when yield curve parallel shifts ONE TIME.

3.       Treasury bond are positive convex bond. positive Convexity push prices MORE up when interest rate goes down, and convexity makes prices go down less when interest go up. callable bond is negative convex bond.

 

Macaulay Duration

The Macaulay duration is the weighted average term to maturity of the cash flows from a bond.

 

Modified Duration

Adjusted modified duration = Macaulay duration/(1+YTM).  It's good for pricing option free bond.

 

Effective Duration

Effective duration- It considers that future interest rate changes that will affect the expected cash flows so it's used for bonds with embedded options like callable bond.

 

Portfolio Duration

Portfolio duration is the weighted average of individual duration of each bond.

 

Key Rate Duration

Since yield curve rarely shifts in parallel so for non-parallel shifts, we need to know key interest rates at different time intervals. Key Rate Duration measures the portfolio’s change of value for change in these key interest rates in the yield curve. It is particularly useful to model bonds with embedded call/prepayment options (like MBS- mortgage-backed securities).

 

Spread Duration

Spread duration measures the change in a bond market value for a one percent parallel shift in its spread above a risk-free treasury bond yield.

 

Sector Duration

Like spread duration, Sector duration measures the change in a sector’s market value for a one percent parallel shift in its spread above a risk-free treasury bond yield.

 

Dollar Duration

It's a change in bond value in dollar terms for every 1% (100 bps) change in its yield.

 

Dollar Duration = -(effective duration) (0.01) Price

 

Dollar duration of portfolio

It's simply the sum of dollar duration of individual bonds with in a portfolio.

 

Present Value Distribution of Cash Flows (PVD)

It is the proportion of a portfolio’s total duration attributable to cash flows (coupons and redemptions) within a selected period.
