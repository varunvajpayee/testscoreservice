2.     Selection of a Benchmark Bond Index

 

General Considerations: Both portfolio and benchmark should have same following risks: -

 

1.       Market value risk- Portfolio Sensitivity to interest rate should match with index.

2.       Income risk - Cash flow timing and amount should match with index

3.       Credit risk- Credit Rating and default risk should match.

4.       Liability framework risk (Asset/Liability structure should match with Index)

 

3.     Aligning the risk factors between portfolio and benchmark

 

1.       Effective duration

2.       Key rate duration

3.       Spread duration

4.       Sector duration

5.       Sector and quality (Bond's rating like BBB, AA,A)

6.       Sector/coupon/maturity cell weights.

7.       Issuer Exposure

 

Note- Duration related risk factors are termed as primary risk factors (large risk factors).

 

 

Points

 

1.       Long Duration portfolio are more sensitive to interest rate change than shorter duration portfolio.

2.       Total Return of Bind = Coupon Income + Reinvestment income + Market Price Increase
